Part 1: Sarf Warm-up
10 minutes · المبني للمجهول (Strong + Weak)
▼

This week focuses on المبني للمجهول. You will train two core passive paradigms before reading: a strong verb (عُلِمَ / يُعْلَمُ) and a hollow waw-verb (قِيلَ / يُقَالُ). Track how the agent disappears and the sentence shifts toward نائب الفاعل.

💡 Pattern 1: Passive of the strong verb In the passive perfect: first radical takes ḍammah and middle radical kasrah (عُلِمَ). In the passive imperfect: prefix takes ḍammah and penultimate takes fatḥah (يُعْلَمُ).

Drill A — Conjugate عُلِمَ:

الضميرالماضي المجهولالمضارع المجهولالأمر
هُوَ عُلِمَ —
هُمْ يُعْلَمُونَ —
أَنْتَ عُلِمْتَ —
💡 Pattern 2: Passive of the hollow waw verb — قَالَ The underlying passive perfect is قُوِلَ, but it contracts to قِيلَ. In the imperfect passive it appears as يُقَالُ.

Drill B — Conjugate قِيلَ / يُقَالُ:

الضميرالماضي المجهولالمضارع المجهولالأمر
هُوَ قِيلَ —
هِيَ تُقَالُ —
هُمْ قِيلُوا —
نَحْنُ نُقَالُ —

Part 3: I'rab & Sarf Quiz
30 minutes · 8 questions
▼

Test your analysis. 4 I'rab questions + 4 Sarf questions.

1
In يُسْتَنْبَطُ الحُكْمُ, what is the I'rab of الحكم?
The verb is passive. So who carries rafʿ now?
A
فاعل مرفوع
B
نائب فاعل مرفوع
C
مفعول به منصوب
D
مبتدأ مرفوع
الحكمُ is نائب فاعل because the verb يُستنبطُ is explicitly passive. In passive clauses, the original agent is omitted and the object is promoted to the نائب الفاعل position. That promoted noun must be مرفوع, just like a regular فاعل. This is the core recognition skill for unvowelled legal prose: no explicit doer, but a raised noun right after the passive verb.
2
Why does the hollow verb appear as قِيلَ in passive perfect, not قُوِلَ in final surface form?
Track the vowel pressure around the weak middle radical.
A
Because every passive must have kasrah on the first radical
B
Because the weak middle letter contracts, yielding the attested form قِيلَ
C
Because قِيلَ is active voice, not passive
D
Because the root is actually ق ي ل
For أجوف verbs, classical derivation discusses an underlying passive shape such as قُوِلَ, then a phonological contraction to the fluent Arabic output قِيلَ. The root remains ق و ل; no root letter is replaced by guesswork. This is exactly why students must separate underlying morphophonemics from final recited form. In text reading, the surface form قيل already signals passive voice and should trigger a search for نائب الفاعل.
3
In والنص ما لا يحتمل إلا معنى واحدا, what is معنى?
What does يحتمل govern here?
A
فاعل مرفوع
B
نائب فاعل مرفوع
C
مفعول به منصوب
D
حال منصوب
معنى is the direct object of يحتمل, so it is مفعول به منصوب. The restriction particle إلا narrows what can be carried: one meaning only. This sentence is a definition of نص, so semantic precision matters — one admissible meaning, no alternatives. Recognising this object position helps distinguish active definitional clauses from passive ones in the same paragraph.
4
Which pair correctly gives passive perfect + passive imperfect for the strong verb عَلِمَ?
Look for the passive vowel signatures.
A
عَلِمَ / يَعْلَمُ
B
عُلِمَ / يُعْلَمُ
C
عُلِمَ / يَعْلَمُ
D
عَلَمَ / يُعْلِمُ
The passive for a sound triliteral uses a stable pattern: perfect فُعِلَ and imperfect يُفْعَلُ. So عَلِمَ becomes عُلِمَ, and يَعْلَمُ becomes يُعْلَمُ. Option A is active voice, option C mixes active and passive, and option D shifts into a different verb family altogether. Mastering this shape lets you spot passive forms even when text is mostly unvowelled.
5
In وهو حجة شرعية, why is شرعية also marfūʿ?
What does a نعت do to its ending?
A
Because it is مفعول به
B
Because it is نعت following حجة in case, gender, and definiteness
C
Because it is بدل from وهو
D
Because every adjective is always marfūʿ
In Arabic syntax, a نعت must match its modified noun. Since حجة is the predicate and sits in a raised slot, شرعية follows that same syntactic case. Adjectives do not have a fixed case by themselves; they mirror their head noun. This agreement system is critical in legal prose where one noun may carry multiple precise qualifiers.
6
What clue most strongly signals a passive construction in unvowelled text?
Use the Week 2 key method.
A
Absence of an explicit doer + a nearby noun behaving like نائب فاعل
B
Any sentence beginning with الواو is passive
C
Every مرفوع noun after a verb is always فاعل
D
Presence of a preposition before the verb
In unvowelled script, you often infer passive by structure rather than visible vowels. If the sentence lacks a named agent and a following noun clearly carries the action's burden, that noun is likely نائب فاعل. This pattern is especially common in usul writing where procedures and rulings are foregrounded. Context plus syntactic role is the reliable method, not surface guesswork.
7
In يُسْتَنْبَطُ, what is the form and voice?
Identify the augmentation first, then the voice.
A
Form I active (يَفْعُلُ)
B
Form X (اِسْتَفْعَلَ) in passive imperfect
C
Form IV active (يُفْعِلُ)
D
Passive perfect from Form X
يُستنبطُ is from Form X: اِسْتَنْبَطَ. The prefix and template signal المضارع, while the internal vocalisation reflects passive voice. So the meaning becomes "is derived" rather than "derives." This is a perfect Week 2 bridge: Sarf form recognition directly controls I'rab analysis of the following noun.
8
Which pair is the correct passive pair for the hollow verb from root ق و ل?
Week 2 warm-up pair.
A
قِيلَ / يُقَالُ
B
قَالَ / يَقُولُ
C
قُوِلَ / يَقَالُ
D
قِيلَ / يَقُولُ
The pedagogically stable pair to internalise is قِيلَ / يُقَالُ. Perfect and imperfect are both passive, and both keep the same weak root ق و ل. Option B is active voice, while options C and D mix malformed or mismatched patterns. If you can map this pair instantly, you will detect passive weak verbs much faster in dense legal passages.

Part 4: Grammar Connection — Qatr al-Nada
25 minutes · الفاعل ونائب الفاعل
▼

Connect your Week 2 analysis to Ibn Hisham's formal definitions in Qatr al-Nada.

قطر الندى — الفاعل ونائب الفاعل
الفاعل هو الاسم المرفوع الذي ذكر قبله فعل أو ما في معناه ونائب الفاعل هو الاسم المرفوع الذي لم يذكر معه فاعله وأسند إليه فعل مبني لما لم يسم فاعله
Translation "The doer (فاعل) is the raised noun preceded by a verb (or what functions like it). The deputy doer (نائب الفاعل) is the raised noun to which a passive verb is ascribed when the original doer is omitted."

Where the rule appears in this week's passage:

ConceptArabicWeek 2 Example
فاعل
Explicit doer		 اللَّهُ In صلى الله عليه وسلم, the verb is active and اللهُ is the overt doer. This is the baseline pattern before any passive shift.
نائب فاعل
Promoted subject in passive		 الْحُكْمُ In يستنبط الحكم, the action is passive: the original actor is omitted, and الحكمُ takes the raised slot as نائب الفاعل.
مفعول به
Direct object in active clause		 مَعْنًى In لا يحتمل إلا معنى واحدا, the verb is active and معنى remains in object position. It is not promoted because the clause is not passive.
Recognition in unvowelled text
Inference skill		 يستنبط الحكم Even without full vowels, you infer passive through structure: no explicit doer + one central raised noun carrying the event.

📌 The big insight Don’t ask only “what ending is this?” Ask “where is the doer?” If no explicit doer appears and the verb is passive by pattern/context, the raised noun is نائب الفاعل.

Week 2 Key Takeaways

I'rab patterns to internalise
  • In passive clauses, the raised noun after the verb is often نائب الفاعل, not فاعل.
  • Check verb voice first, then assign role: passive voice reshuffles argument structure.
  • مفعول به remains in active clauses (e.g., يحتمل معنى).
  • Adjectives still track their nouns in case, even in dense legal definitions.
Sarf patterns to internalise
  • Strong passive core pair: فُعِلَ / يُفْعَلُ → عُلِمَ / يُعْلَمُ.
  • Hollow passive contraction: underlying قُوِلَ surfaces as قِيلَ.
  • Passive weak verbs are best tracked by pattern families, not memorised in isolation.
  • Form recognition (e.g., يُسْتَنْبَطُ) directly supports I'rab accuracy.
