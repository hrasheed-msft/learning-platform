Part 1: Sarf Warm-up
10 minutes · أفعال المثال الواوي
▼

This week drills الفعل المثال الواوي. Notice how the initial واو stays in past tense (وَعَدَ) but drops in imperfect and imperative (يَعِدُ · عِدْ).

💡 Core rule If the first radical is و and the imperfect has kasrah on the second radical, Arabic drops that initial waw for ease in المضارع and الأمر.

Drill A — Conjugate وَعَدَ:

الضميرالماضيالمضارعالأمر
هُوَوَعَدَ—
هُمْيَعِدُونَ—
أَنْتَوَعَدْتَ
💡 Parallel pattern وَصَلَ behaves the same: يَصِلُ · صِلْ. Past keeps waw, imperfect/imperative drop it.

Drill B — Conjugate وَصَلَ:

الضميرالماضيالمضارعالأمر
هُوَوَصَلَ—
هِيَتَصِلُ—
أَنْتَوَصَلْتَ

Part 4: Grammar Connection — Qatr al-Nada
25 minutes · المبتدأ والخبر
▼

Formalise this week's parsing with Ibn Hisham's discussion of subject-predicate sentences.

قطر الندى — المبتدأ والخبر
المبتدأ اسم مجرد عن العوامل اللفظية مسند إليه والخبر هو المسند الذي تتم به الفائدة وقد يتقدم الخبر إذا كان ظرفا أو جارا ومجرورا
Translation “The مبتدأ is a noun free of verbal governors to which predication is attached; the خبر is the predicate that completes meaning. The predicate may be fronted when it is a ظرف or prepositional phrase.”

Week 3 Key Takeaways

I'rab patterns to internalise
  • Detect خبر مقدم when a PP opens the sentence.
  • Keep مبتدأ/خبر mapping explicit before interpreting legal hierarchy.
  • Nominal definitions in usul depend on clean predicate structure.
Sarf patterns to internalise
  • In المثال الواوي, past keeps waw: وَعَدَ.
  • Imperfect + imperative often drop waw: يَعِدُ · عِدْ.
  • The root is unchanged; only surface realization shifts.
