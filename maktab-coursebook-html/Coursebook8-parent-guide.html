Naw\ﬁl

📝 Lesson Summary

Ṣalāh Allāh has made the ﬁve daily ṣalāh compulsory upon us. Ṣalāh can be thought of as 'meetings and conversations' with the King of the universe. It is a time when we get a chance to talk to our Creator and Provider. Ṣalāh is a chance to seek the mercy and help of Allāh . Ṣalāh is a chance to become a friend of the Ruler of the universe. The Beloved Messenger had the most love for Allāh . He showed and taught us how to get even closer to Allāh and how to be Allāh 's closest friends.

Key Points:

  • Ṣalāh Allāh has made the ﬁve daily ṣalāh compulsory upon us. Ṣalāh can be thought of as 'meetings and conversations' with the King of the universe.
  • The Beloved Messenger had the most love for Allāh .
  • He showed and taught us how to get even closer to Allāh and how to be Allāh 's closest friends.
  • From among the ways to become much closer to Allāh is by performing extra ṣalāh.
  • The Messenger of Allāh said, "Allāh, the Exalted, has said: 'I will declare war against he who harms my pious worshippers.

Nikāḥ

📝 Lesson Summary

Nikāḥ (marriage) is a great sunnah in Islām. When a man and a woman marry, love, peace and companionship is created between them. The Prophet equated marriage to half of one's faith. He said: "Whoever marries has completed (by protecting) half of his faith: let him fear violating the other half." (Ṭabarān|) The purpose of marriage A Muslim should marry because: 1)Marriage is a form of worshipping Allāh . 2)It is the sunnah (way) of the Prophets . 3)A human being naturally needs to marry.

Key Points:

  • Nikāḥ (marriage) is a great sunnah in Islām.
  • When a man and a woman marry, love, peace and companionship is created between them.
  • The Prophet equated marriage to half of one's faith.
  • The Prophet said: "Women are (usually) married for four reasons: for their wealth, ancestry, beauty and piety.
  • Marry the pious one and you will be successful." (Ṣahīh al-Bukhārī) From all the different qualities in a person, piety is the most desirable quality.

Ṭalāq

📝 Lesson Summary

FiqhFiqh Everybody has ups and downs. Brothers and sisters have good days and bad days together. Likewise, a husband and wife may experience some good days and some bad days. Anytime you experience anger in your marriage, extinguish the anger by: •Drinking a glass of water •Changing your position to a lower state: If you are standing, sit down.

Key Points:

  • This is known as ṭalāq (divorce).
  • If by chance she gets married to another person and then gets divorced, she may re-marry her ﬁrst husband after the waiting period (iddah) is over.
  • After a ṭalāq raj'|, if the 'iddah (waiting period) has not ended then a new marriage is not required to get back together.
  • In the waiting period, a woman is not permitted to marry anyone besides the husband who gave her ṭalāq bā'in.
  • After the 'iddah is complete, she is free to marry whomever she wishes.

Buyū'

📝 Lesson Summary

"The truthful merchant is rewarded by being ranked on the Day of Resurrection with the prophets, the truthful elite and the martyrs." (Tirmidhī) There are some conditions which must be present at the time of buying and selling items: 1)The product must be ḥalāl. It is not permissible to sell ḥarām items. For example, it is not permissible to sell alcohol, stolen goods, pork etc. 2)The price and the item for sale must be clearly speciﬁed at the time of the transaction.

Key Points:

  • It is not permissible to sell ḥarām items.
  • For example, Mūsā sells a watch to Idrīs on the condition that Idrīs gives Mūsā the watch to wear once a week.
  • This will not be permissible.

Interest is charging money for giving someone a loan. ĪFor example, if Yūsuf wanted a loan of 10,000, the bank only gives it to him on condition that Yūsuf ĪĪpays back 12,000. The extra 2,000 is interest and is ḥarām to give and take. Through interest, the rich become richer and the poor become poorer. Allāh states in the Qur'ān: "Those who consume interest shall not stand up on the Day of Resurrection except like him who Shayṭān has driven mad by his touch.

Key Points:

  • Ribā means interest.
  • The extra 2,000 is interest and is ḥarām to give and take.
  • That is because they say: "Trade is just like interest." But Allāh has permitted trade and has forbidden interest.
  • So whoever has received an admonition from his Lord and desists may have that (amount) which has passed, and his affair rests with Allāh.
  • What is meant by 'destroys interest' is that Allāh deprives the money generated by interest of all blessings.
