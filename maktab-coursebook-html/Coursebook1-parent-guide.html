Shahādah

📝 Lesson Summary

The ﬁrst and most important pillar of Islām is the shahādah. What is the shahādah? The shahādah is to believe and say that there is no god but Allāh and our Beloved Messenger Muḥammad is His servant and messenger. The words of shahādah are: I believe that there is no god but Allāh , and I believe that Muḥammad is His servant and messenger.

Key Points:

Doing Things Calmly

📝 Lesson Summary

Rasūlullāh said: Al anātu minallāhi wal 'ajalatu minash shayṭāni “Doing things calmly is from Allāh and doing things in a hurry is from Shayṭān.” (Tirmidhī) If we do something in a hurry, we are more likely to make mistakes and we will not be able to do it correctly. 📝 Lesson Summary

Our Beloved Messenger Muḥammad was born in the city of Makkah, early on a Monday morning of Rabī' al-Awwal. The year he was born was known as 'The Year of the Elephant' as it was in this year that Abrahah, the governor of Yemen, decided to build his own place of worship. Abrahah also decided to attack the Ka'bah with his army of elephants because he wanted people to visit his place of worship instead of the Ka'bah in Makkah.

Ādāb of Using

📝 Lesson Summary

the Washroom Anas reports that when the h would \ Messenger of All enter the washroom he would say: Oh Allāh I seek refuge in You from all evil and evil doers. (Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī) the Washroom Cover your hair. Enter with your left foot. Do not face your front or back towards the Qiblah. Sit and use the toilet.

Key Points:

  • Enter with your left foot.
  • Do not face your front or back towards the Qiblah.
  • Sit and use the toilet.
  • You should not stand and use the toilet.
