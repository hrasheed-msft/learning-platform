Keywords

Actions in Islām are placed in different groups.

Some are far}, some are sunnah whilst others are mustaḥabb. There are also some which are makrūh and others which are nāqi}. So what do these words mean?

1.Farā'iḍ: Plural of far}. Far} is something that you have to do. By leaving a far} action out, the act remains incomplete.

2.Sunan: Plural of sunnah. Sunnah is an act which our Beloved Messenger Muḥammad practised and carried out. By following the sunnah, we will get a lot of reward. In ﬁqh, it is a stage after far} and wājib. If a person misses a sunnah out, the action will still be done but the reward will be less.

3.Mustaḥabbāt: Plural of mustaḥabb.

Mustaḥabb is an act that is good for us to do and is beloved in Islām. We will get extra reward for doing mustaḥabb actions.

4.Makrūhāt: Plural of makrūh. Makrūh is something that is disliked in Islām. We should stay away from anything that is makrūh.

5.Nawāqiḍ: Plural of nāqi}. Nawāqiḍ are those actions that break other actions.

Just after our Beloved Messenger had received the ﬁrst revelation and was quite shocked, Khadījah said, "Never, Allāh will never disgrace you: you keep good relations with your kith and kin (close family relatives), and you help the poor and the destitute (someone who is very poor)." (Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī) The Ṣa#ābah would compete with one another to help other people who might be in need.

Even when 'Umar was not the khalīfah, it was his practice to move about in Madīnah and help people in distress. In one of the outskirts of Madīnah there lived an old woman who was unable to see and had no one to help her. 'Umar used to go in disguise to the house of the old woman, but was always surprised to ﬁnd that someone else had visited before him, to help the old lady.

'Umar felt distressed that in this noble task of helping a lady in distress some other person was reaching her before him. One day, 'Umar went to the house of the old woman earlier than usual and hid himself to watch as to who the Akhlāq person was that attended to the needs of the old woman.

'Umar did not have to wait long, for soon a man arrived to help the old woman. This man was none other than the khalīfah Abū Bakr .

Sub#ānallāh, he was the leader but he still went round to help people in need.

Kindness to Animals

Our Beloved Messenger said "Fear Allāh regarding these mute animals: mount them when they are ﬁt (they are fully rested), and dismount them while they are still ﬁt (before tiring them out)." (Abū Dāwūd) Our Beloved Messenger commanded us to be kind to animals. We must not hurt any animal.

Animals are also the Creation of Allāh . The Ṣa#ābah asked Muḥammad "Is there a reward for us in serving (the) animal?" He replied, "Yes, there is a reward for serving every living creature." (Mālik)

There lived a lady who had done many things in her life that were wrong. One day she was out in the hot desert and saw a dog panting out of thirst. The dog was so thirsty that his tongue was hanging out. This lady thought of a way to help this poor animal. There was a well nearby but there was no bucket, so she removed her shoe and tied it to the length of her headscarf. She then lowered the shoe into the well to get some water. When it was full she carefully made the thirsty dog drink. Allāh was so happy with this one action of hers, showing kindness to one of Allāh 's Creation, that He forgave all her sins and granted her Jannah.

Dear children, if we ever see any animal that may be hurt or in pain we should try our best to help. We must never hurt any animal. Even when we are riding an animal we should do so without making it too tired.

There is a story of another lady who used to pray a lot. This lady had kept a cat as a pet, but she did not look after it. Instead, she would lock the poor cat up, causing it to starve. She was so cruel to this cat that it eventually died.

Allāh was upset with this lady who had hurt one of His Creation. He wiped her good actions away and punished her. She will go to Jahannam for hurting one of Allāh 's Creation. May Allāh allow us to care for all that He has created. Āmīn.

Ādāb of Greeting

When you are greeted with a greeting, greet with one better than it, or return the same. Surely, Allāh is the One who takes account of everything. While greeting one another, remember the following important actions:

Greet the people you know and also those who you do not know by saying 'Assalāmu 'Alaykum.' It is you who should greet ﬁrst, especially in these situations: When passing or meeting an elder. When walking past those that are sitting. When walking by a larger group.

Greet every time you meet someone, even if a small separation has occurred.

Greet every time you enter the house.

Greet loud enough for people to hear.

If a person informs you that such-and- such a person has sent you salām, reply with the following words: When a male sends you salām:

Entering a House and Seeking Permission , …When you enter homes, greet one another with salām, a greeting prescribed by Allāh which is blessed and pleasant. Knock no more than three times.

Entering a House and Seeking Permission Whilst waiting between the knocks do not stand in front of the door. Instead, stand to the right or the left. Our Beloved Messenger Muḥammad would stand to the right or the left when coming to someone's door. (Abū Dāwūd) Do not start looking inside the house, through the windows or the letter box.

Identify yourself. Do not say "It's me," but instead say your name.

Enter with your right foot.

Say 'salām' upon entering. If you are entering a house wherein people may be resting, do not raise your voice. If you are entering your own home or a house to Ādāb which you have the keys, then again, say salām. It will let the people inside the house know you are there so they do not become shocked or startled.

Never enter another person's house or room without permission.
