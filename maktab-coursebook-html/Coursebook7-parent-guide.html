Sutrah

📝 Lesson Summary

We should never walk directly across a person performing ṣalāh, i.e. between that person and the direction of Qiblah. The Prophet said: "If the one crossing in front of a praying person knew what sin is upon him, it would be better for him to wait for forty than walk across." Abū An-Naḍr (one of the narrators of the ḥadīth) said: "I don't know whether he said forty days, months, or years.

Key Points:

Qaṣr Ṣalāh

📝 Lesson Summary

If a person has an intention of travelling more than 54 miles (87 km), they will be regarded as a traveller (mus\ﬁr) in Islām. A traveller can shorten their ṣalāh, performing it as what is called 'qaṣr'. Qaṣr is to perform two rak'āt far} for Ḍhuhr, 'Aṣr and 'Ishā' instead of four. A person will be eligible to perform qaṣr the moment they leave the boundaries of their home town up until they reach back to the boundaries of their home town.

Key Points:

  • A traveller can shorten their ṣalāh, performing it as what is called 'qaṣr'.
  • Qaṣr is to perform two rak'āt far} for Ḍhuhr, 'Aṣr and 'Ishā' instead of four.
  • If a person has an intention of travelling more than 54 miles (87 km), they will be regarded as a traveller (mus\ﬁr) in Islām.
  • Furthermore if a person intends to stay at a place for less than 15 days, they too will perform qaṣr at the destination.

Ṣalātul Ma'dhūr

📝 Lesson Summary

At times, people suffer from illnesses whereby they cannot keep their wuḍū' at all. For example, a bleeding wound which doesn't stop bleeding at all during an entire ṣalāh time. If a person experiences the problem from the entry time of any far} ṣalāh until the expiry time of the ṣalāh in such a way that they are unable to ﬁnd enough time required to make wuḍū' and perform the far} ṣalāh without the problem occurring, they will then be regarded as a ma'dhūr.

Key Points:

  • Once a person is regarded as a ma'dhūr, the illness must continue at least once in every ṣalāh time for this condition to remain in place.
  • Once a person is regarded as a ma'dhūr, the occurrence of the illness will not break the wuḍū'.
  • At times, people suffer from illnesses whereby they cannot keep their wuḍū' at all.

Zakāh

📝 Lesson Summary

Zakāh is a monetary act of worship that has been ordained in the shar|'ah. It is one of the ﬁve pillars of Islām. Zakāh literally means 'to increase' and it also means 'to purify'. Both of these meanings are found through the action of zakāh.

Key Points:

  • Zakāh is a monetary act of worship that has been ordained in the shar|'ah.
  • It is one of the ﬁve pillars of Islām.
  • Zakāh literally means 'to increase' and it also means 'to purify'.
  • Both of these meanings are found through the action of zakāh.
  • Allāh puriﬁes us and our wealth and furthermore grants barakah in our wealth and increases our reward.

I'tikāf

📝 Lesson Summary

I'tikāf means to seclude oneself and stay in the Masjid. 'Ā'ishah narrates that the Beloved Messen- ger Muḥammad would perform I'tikāf during the last ten days of Ramaḍān until Allāh took him away, then his wives performed I'tikāf after him. (Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī) Abū Hurayrah narrates that the Qur'an would be recited to the Beloved Messenger once every year. It was recited to him twice the year he passed away.

Key Points:

Uḍḥiyah

📝 Lesson Summary

Zayd ibn Arqam reports that the Companions of Rasūlullāh asked him: "Ya Rasūlallāh, what is this sacriﬁce?" He said: "It is the way of your forefather Ibrāhīm ." They asked: "What (reward) is for us therein?" He replied: "There is a reward for every hair." They asked: "For the wool, Ya Rasūlallāh ?" He replied: "There is one reward for every strand of wool." (Aḥmad) Uḍḥiyah refers to the animal sacriﬁce offered in the days of Ḥajj. This is also commonly called 'qurbān|'.

Key Points:

  • This is also commonly called 'qurbān|'.
  • The time of slaughtering depends where the slaughtering is taking place.
  • If the slaughtering is taking place in a city, then the slaughtering of qurbān| can only begin after 'Īd Ṣalāh.
  • If the slaughtering takes place in a village, provided there is no 'Īd Ṣalāh, slaughtering can begin after the break of dawn (Sub# a-]ādiq).
  • The animals which can be sacriﬁced are the following: 1)Sheep 2)Goats 3)Cattle 4)Camels The animals can be male or female.
