Masa# 'alal Khuffayn

📝 Lesson Summary

Khuffayn are a special type of socks upon which one can do masa# (wipe wet hands) rather than wash the feet in wuḍū'. Conditions for the Khuff In order to do masa# upon a khuff, the following conditions must be met: 1.The khuff must be worn after washing the feet in wuḍū' before wuḍū' is broken again. 2.The khuff must cover the feet up to (and including) the ankles. 3.

Key Points:

  • Khuffayn are a special type of socks upon which one can do masa# (wipe wet hands) rather than wash the feet in wuḍū'.
  • It is permissible to do masa# over mujalladayn (leather on top and bottom of the socks).
  • It is also permissible to do masa# over muna'alayn (leather only on the bottom and thick on top).
  • It is impermissible to perform masa# on non-leather thin socks like the cotton and nylon socks we commonly wear.

Masa# on Wounds

📝 Lesson Summary

Even when a person is wounded, he still has to perform ṣalāh. Islām has given us ways to perform wuḍū' and ghusl despite being injured. If a wound can be washed without any harm by removing the bandage or plaster, then a person must wash the wound and perform wuḍū' or ghusl as normal. If the wound cannot be washed but can be wiped without any harm then one should wipe the wound.

Key Points:

  • Even when a person is wounded, he still has to perform ṣalāh.

Sajdah as-Sahw

📝 Lesson Summary

Feeding Others

📝 Lesson Summary

Rasūlullāh said: "The most virtuous charity is feeding a hungry creature." (Bayhaq|) Charity in Arabic is known as ṣadaqah. In another ḥadīth Rasūlullāh said, "Hasten towards giving ṣadaqah for calamities cannot come to a person who gives ṣadaqah." Here Rasūlullāh is telling us that to feed the hungry is the best type of ṣadaqah a person can do. The word 'creature' includes all those that are alive, which means that even if you feed a hungry animal you will get this reward.

Key Points:

  • Rasūlullāh said: "The most virtuous charity is feeding a hungry creature." (Bayhaq|) Charity in Arabic is known as ṣadaqah.
  • The word 'creature' includes all those that are alive, which means that even if you feed a hungry animal you will get this reward.
  • Feeding anyone is a great deed in itself, but when someone is in need of food, at such a time it will be more rewarding.
  • We should always try our best to help those who are in need, even if it may be little.
  • We don't know which of our actions is liked by Allāh the most.

Ṣadaqah is known as charity in Islām and our Beloved Messenger Muḥammad said, "Give ṣadaqah without delay, for it stands in the way of calamity.

Key Points:

  • There is a story mentioned in Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī about a person who was walking one day, and saw a branch which was obstructing the pathway.
  • Let us think for a moment: why is there so much importance placed on such a small action?
  • When looking over many a#ādīth, one reason that can be understood is that Allāh loves a person helping another and detests a person who hurts another.
  • Imagine you are walking and you see a banana peel in the middle of the pathway: you pick it up and place it in the bin.
  • We sometimes forget that others will walk after us on the road we are strolling along.
