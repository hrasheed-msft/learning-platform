Fiqh

Learning Objectives

At the end of this chapter pupils will be able to:

Summarise the ﬁve pillars of Islam and develop an understanding of each pillar.

Apply ṭahārah and understand its importance in everyday life.

Identify and practise the method of wuḍū’.

The Pillars of Islām Shahādah Ṣalāh Zakāh Ṣawm Ḥajj

The Pillars of Islām Imagine a house with very strong walls.

No matter how much it rains or how windy it is, the house will stay strong.

You can push as hard as you want, but the house will just not move!

Now, imagine yourself as that strong house!

Stand up and stay strong! Keep your legs straight and ﬁxed to the ground. If someone pushes you, will you fall down? Just like this strong house, Islām also has strong walls which are called pillars. These pillars are called the 'Pillars of Islam'.

There are ﬁve pillars of Islam. If we keep these pillars strong in our life, our Islām will never fall down!

Shahādah

The ﬁrst and most important pillar of Islām is the shahādah.

What is the shahādah? The shahādah is to believe and say that there is no god but Allāh and our Beloved Messenger Muḥammad is His servant and messenger.

The words of shahādah are:

I believe that there is no god but Allāh , and I believe that Muḥammad is His servant and messenger.

Ṣalāh

Ṣalāh is the second pillar of Islām. It is a special way Muslims pray to Allāh ﬁve times a day.

Ṣalāh is a gift from Allāh .

Ṣalāh is a way of asking Allāh .

Ṣalāh is a way of thanking Allāh . If we pray ṣalāh everyday, Allāh will be happy with us and give us jannah. The ﬁve ṣalāh are:

FajrḌhuhr'AṣrMaghrib'Ishā'

Here is a little poem for you to remember the times and names of the ﬁve daily ṣalāh:

Fajr in the morning Ḍhuhr after midday 'Aṣr in the afternoon Maghrib when the sun goes down 'Ishā' before we sleep

Zakāh

The third pillar of Islām is zakāh.

Zakāh is giving money to those in need.

Allāh has told us to give a little amount of money once a year to the people in need.

Zakāh teaches us to care.

Zakāh teaches us to share. If we give zakāh, Allāh will be happy with us and give us more!

Ṣawm

The fourth pillar of Islām is ṣawm.

Ṣawm is the Arabic word for fasting.

Fasting is when a person does not eat or drink during the day.

Allāh has told us to fast in the month of Ramaḍān.

Fasting teaches us how the poor people feel.

Fasting teaches us to be thankful.

Fasting teaches us to share. If we fast, we can become close friends of Allāh .

Ḥajj

The ﬁfth pillar of Islām is #ajj.

Ḥajj is a special journey to the city of Makkah. A Muslim who has enough money for the journey and is able to go, must do #ajj at least once in their life. In Makkah there is a special building called the ka’bah. We face the ka’bah when we pray our ṣalāh ﬁve times a day. In #ajj, we go around the ka’bah seven times. This is known as ṭawāf. Ḥajj has many other actions too, taught to us by Rasūlullāh . After #ajj, muslims go to Madīnah Munawwarah to visit the blessed city of Rasūlullāh . This is known as ziyārah.

Ṭahārah

For us to pray ṣalāh, we must have ṭahārah.

Ṭahārah means to be clean. We all like clean things, clean places and to be clean.

Allāh loves clean people.

Rasūlullāh taught and showed us how to be clean all the time. We must wash and take a bath regularly. We should clean ourselves properly in the washroom. If we do this we will be loved by Allāh .

Wuḍū'

Allāh has taught us a special way to clean ourselves before we stand in ṣalāh. This special way is called wuḍū‘.

Whoever does wuḍū‘ properly all the time, his face, hands and feet will shine brightly on the Day of Judgement.

How to do wuḍū'

We say 'Bismillāh' to start. We then wash both hands till the wrist. We rinse our mouth three times. We rinse our nose three times with the right hand and then clean it once with the left hand.

We then wash our full face three times, from the top of the forehead to the bottom of the chin and from one ear to the other.

Next we wash both arms three times, including the elbows, starting with the right. We pass the ﬁngers of each hand through the other hand. This is called khilāl.

Wuḍū'

We pass wet hands on our head all the way to the back. This is called 'masa#'. We wipe our ears inside and out using our index ﬁngers and then we use our thumbs to wipe the back of our ears.

Finally, we wash our feet three times, starting with the right, including the ankles. We then do khilāl of our toes.

Feeding the Hungry

Feeding the Hungry & Visiting the Sick ََْْRasūlullāh said:

A{'imul jāi'a wa 'ūdul mar|}a “Feed the hungry and visit the sick.” (Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī)

Feeding the Hungry & Visiting the Sick Allāh has blessed us in so many ways. If we think about the things that Allāh has given us, we will not be able to count them all. In this ḥadīth, Rasūlullāh is reminding us to feed the hungry so we can share what we have. Can you remember a time when you felt really hun- gry? How happy did you feel after you had eaten your food?

Dear children, there are many people in the world who are hungry and we must try to help them. The second lesson that Rasūlullāh is giving us in this ḥadīth is to visit the sick. When a person is not well they are usually in bed, unable to go out and meet other people like they normally would do. When someone comes to visit them it makes them very happy and to make someone happy is a good deed. If we hear someone is not well, we should visit them and if we can't, then we should at least call them. By following these two lessons we will learn to care and be grateful.

Helping Others

(al-Mu’jam al-Awsaṭ)

To help someone in need is a good action. By helping someone, we can make them happy. In this ḥadīth, Rasūllullāh is teaching us that the best people are those that help others.

There are so many ways we can be helpful. Can you think of some?

Here are some ideas you can try this week:

  • Clean your room.
  • Tidy up your toys.
  • Pick up some litter.
  • Help wash the dishes.
  • Explain the lesson to someone.

Doing Things Calmly

Rasūlullāh said:

Al anātu minallāhi wal 'ajalatu minash shayṭāni “Doing things calmly is from Allāh and doing things in a hurry is from Shayṭān.” (Tirmidhī)

If we do something in a hurry, we are more likely to make mistakes and we will not be able to do it correctly. In this ḥadīth, Rasūlullāh is teaching us that doing things calmly is something that Allāh loves, and doing things in a hurry is something that shayṭān likes. For example, we want to eat our food because we are very hungry: if we eat quickly we may choke, cough, or even get a stomach ache, if we take our time and chew the food properly, we will be able to eat and enjoy the food too.

Always remember to do things calmly!

Think of other things that can go wrong when we do things in a hurry.

Staying Clean

َْRasūlullāh said:َّْ Aṭ ṭuhūru shaṭrul īmāni “Purity is half of īmān.” (Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim)

It is so important for us to stay clean that Rasūlullāh said it is "half of īmān."

Īmān is what we believe in. Īmān is the key to jannah. We must keep our body and clothes clean, our house, our masjid and our surroundings clean. A clean person is loved by Allāh , the angels and all the people. It is easy to stay clean if we try. By staying clean, half of our īmān is complete.

Think of all the ways we can stay clean.

Speaking the Truth

Rasūlullāh said:ْ 'Alaykum bi ṣidqi “Hold on to the truth.” (Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim)

Our Beloved Messenger Muḥammad has told us to hold on to the truth. When something is important, we always hold on to it and refuse to let go. Just like that, we must never let the truth go, and we must stay away from lying. We must always speak the truth.

'Abdul Qādir was a young boy who went to Baghdād to learn. To set him on his way, his mum gave him some money and told him, “My dear son, never lie! whatever happens to you.” One day a gang of robbers stopped the caravan and began asking all the people what they had. When 'Abdul Qādir’s turn came, he told them that he had forty gold coins. The robbers then took him to their leader. When the leader asked why he had spoken the truth, 'Abdul Qādir told the leader of the gang what his mum had taught him. The leader was so shocked by this answer that he began crying and said that,“this boy is so young, but he listens to his mum, whilst I am so old and don’t even listen to Allāh .” He changed his ways and gave the money back to all the people.

'Abdul Qādir became a very great friend of Allāh when he grew up. We too must remember the lesson of speaking the truth at all times.

Childhood of Rasūlullāh

Our Beloved Messenger Muḥammad was born in the city of Makkah, early on a Monday morning of Rabī' al-Awwal. The year he was born was known as 'The Year of the Elephant' as it was in this year that Abrahah, the governor of Yemen, decided to build his own place of worship.

Abrahah also decided to attack the Ka'bah with his army of elephants because he wanted people to visit his place of worship instead of the Ka'bah in Makkah. The Ka'bah is a building in the city of Makkah which Ibrāhīm built for the worship of Allāh . We face the Ka'bah when we pray our ṣalāh ﬁve times a day. It is a very special place where Allāh is remembered.

Allāh protected the Ka'bah and destroyed Abrahah along with his army by sending a group of birds that dropped tiny stones from their beaks.

Muḥammad 's father's name was 'Abdullāh, and his mother's name was Āminah.

Muḥammad was an orphan because his father, 'Abdullāh, had passed away just before Muḥammad was born. It was a habit of the Arabs to send their children to the villages for the ﬁrst few years of their life.

Parents would send their children to the villages so they could grow up learning the pure Arabic language whilst being away from the pollution and busy life of the city.

Women from the villages would come to the city to look after the children.

Ḥalīmah was from the tribe of Ban[ Sīrah Sa'd who lived in a village. She also came to Makkah, along with others, looking for a child to take back with her, so that the child could grow up in the village.

Rasūlullāh 's mother, Āminah, allowed Ḥalīmah to take Rasūlullāh with her back to the village.

Allāh blessed Ḥalīmah through Rasūlullāh . Her animals began to give more milk than ever before and her camel which was weak and slow suddenly became strong and fast.

Ḥalīmah kept Rasūlullāh for four years.

When Rasūlullāh was six years

old, he went with his mother Āminah to visit their relatives in Madīnah. On their way back, Āminah passed away at a place called Abwā'. Umm Ayman, who was travelling with them, brought Rasūlullāh back to Makkah.

Rasūlullāh was cared for by his grandfather, 'Abdul Muṭṭalib who loved Rasūlullāh very much.

'Abdul Muṭṭalib was the leader of Makkah at that time. When Rasūlullāh was eight years old, 'Abdul Muṭṭalib passed away, after which Rasūlullāh was looked after by his uncle, Abū Ṭālib.

Rasūlullāh 's Youth When Rasūlullāh was 12 years old, he went with his uncle Abū Ṭālib, on a journey to Syria. On the way they stopped at a place and were met by a monk whose name was Baḥīrah.

Baḥīrah told Abū Ṭālib that his nephew was going to become the last messenger of Allāh.

Abū Ṭālib asked him how he knew this. Baḥīrah replied that he saw the trees and stones bowing down to Rasūlullāh and they do not do this for any other person except for a messenger of Allāh Baḥīrah also said he had recognised the seal of prophethood between Rasūlullāh 's shoulders.

He told Abū Ṭālib not to take him to Syria

because people would recognise him and might want to hurt him out of jealousy.

Baḥīrah said that he saw the trees bowing down to Muḥammad . As Muḥammad grew up, the people began to see that he had great qualities: he was always honest and trustworthy. They began calling himA-%ādiq(the most honest) andAl-Amīn(the most trustworthy). They would all respect him.

One day the Quraysh decided to rebuild the Ka'bah as its walls had become weak. When the time came to put the 'Black Stone' in its place, they had an argument. They could not decide who should put the Black Stone in the wall of the Ka'bah. They were so angry that they were ready to ﬁght.

However, someone suggested that whoever came into the Masjid next could decide what should be done. Muḥammad was the ﬁrst person to enter. They were all happy and said that:

"He is a-%ādiq and al-Amīn, and we are happy."

Muḥammad told a person from each tribe to come forward and take hold of a piece of cloth with the Black Stone in the middle, and take it to the Ka'bah, after which he lifted it and put in its place.

Marriage to Khadījah

In Makkah there lived a very rich woman whose name was Khadījah . When Khadījah heard how honest and trustworthy Muḥammad was, she asked him to go on a business Sīrah trip to Syria and sell her goods. She sent her servant Maysara to go along with him.

Muḥammad 's trip was very successful for Khadījah . On the way, another monk had told Maysarah that Muḥammad was going to be a prophet.

Khadījah expressed her wish to marry Muḥammad .

Muḥammad was 25 years old when he married Khadījah , while Khadījah 's age was 40.

Allāh blessed them with four daughters and two sons.

Articles of Faith

These are the Articles of Faith that all Muslims must believe in:

Allāh His angels'Aqā'id His books His messengers The Last Day Fate: good and bad is all from Allāh Life after death

Allāh ٌَََُّّ'Aqā'id

Say, Allāh is One. Allāh is A-Ṣamad, (He needs no one and everyone needs Him). He did not give birth nor was He born and no one is equal to Him.

(Qur’ān, Sūrah: 112)

Allāh is our Lord. We are all His servants; He created us and to Him we shall all return.

Allāh is one. This means He is alone and He does not have any partners. He does not have a mum or dad, or brothers or sisters, or any sons or daughters.

Allāh has always been and will always be. We cannot see Allāh but He is watching over each and every one of us all the time.

He was not created by anyone. He does not depend on anybody but everyone depends on Him.

Allāh has created us to worship and obey Him.

Allāh is the Most Kind.

Allāh is the Most Merciful.

Allāh has many qualities.

Allāhhas many names which are known as al-Asm\-ul-Ḥusn\ (The Most Beautiful Names of Allāh ). We shall now discuss some of these qualities of Allāh .

Ar-Razzāq - The Provider This means He gives us everything - from clothes to food and from family to friends. Everything we see around us is provided by Him. We may see that our parents feed us, but let us think: who gave them the strength to work? Who put love for us into their hearts? Who granted them their knowledge? So in reality Allāh is the real provider.

Ar-Razzāq - The Provider One day, the Companions of our Beloved Messenger Muḥammad were on a journey wherein they had no food except dates and water. Each Companion was given one date to eat every day. They then began eating the leaves of trees until they reached the coast where they saw a large object. When they came near it, they saw it was a huge ﬁsh, known as Al ‘Anbar.

Jābir says: “Three hundred of us ate from it for a whole month and then packed its meat for our journey. When we came back to Madīnah, we went to Allah's Messenger and told him, He said: That was food which Allāh had brought out (from the ocean) for you.” (Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim)

Dear children, look how Allāh provided such a large ﬁsh for the Companions of our Beloved Messenger . We must also ask Allāh to provide for us in this world and the ākhirah. Allāh loves it when we ask Him.

Cleanliness

Staying clean is so important.

Cleanliness is loved by Allāh . A clean person is loved by everyone. If we do not look after ourselves we will automatically become unclean. To become clean, we have to put some effort in, we have to wash and we have to scrub. If we don’t, germs and bacteria will come and stay on us and around us.

Our bodies are amazing Each day they are busy working hard doing lots of important things through the will of Allāh , such as digesting food, pumping blood and oxygen.

There is a group of tiny invaders that are invisible to us but if they come they can These tiny invaders are make our bodies ill. called germs.

Some germs are called viruses. If viruses get inside our bodies they can spread and make us unwell. Some viruses only live for a while on something like a door handle or table. The best way to stay clean and away from germs is to wash regularly.

Let’s look at different ways we can make sure we stay clean all the time.

Our Body

Wash your hands before and after meals.

Brush your teeth twice a day. Have a bath regularly.

Wear clean clothes.

Comb your hair.

Wash yourself after you use the washroom.

Make sure your nails are short, as germs can live under your nails.

Around us

See that your toys and books are neatly put away and not left around.

Make your bed as soon as you wake up. Do not eat in your bedroom as this can leave crumbs behind which may invite insects to visit! Be sure to leave things as you found them.

Move anything that may be harmful out of the way.

In the Washroom When using the washroom:

Make sure you sit down completely. Do not stand and use the toilet.

Clean your private parts after using the toilet with tissue paper and water. Our Beloved Messenger Muḥammad has told us about a person who was being punished by Allāh because he did not keep himself clean after urinating. (Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī)

When Eating

Wash your hands before and after the meal. The hands are the best place germs like to stay.

See that no food crumbs fall around you. Do not let food fall onto your clothes.

Water

Using water is the best way we can stay clean!

Use it wisely - do not waste it. If we stay clean we will feel happy too.

Water is a gift to us from Allāh Remember:

Allāh loves those who make themselves clean. (Qur’ān 9:108)

Gentleness in Speech

The tongue is a gift from Allāh . We must think before we use it to speak.

Always think of how we can speak nicely. If we say something which is not nice we may make the other person, who we are talking to, upset. Our Beloved Messenger always spoke gently. He always said nice and kind words to people. We must think how we would feel if someone spoke to us rudely.Akhlāq Let us look at some examples of how we can say something nicely and gently.

Example 1 You have some sweets and you want to share with someone: How should you ask them?

Hey! Do you want one?

Shall I give you one?

Please, take some.

How nice and friendly does the third one sound? You are actually asking them to take some. They will feel so special. If you make someone happy, Allāh will be happy with you.

Example 2 We really want something from an elder.

How should we speak?

Give me that!

2Please, may I have this? Are you going to give me that or not? Which one sounds the most polite?

That’s right. The second sentence.

Example 3 You see your elderly neighbour carrying some bags.

How would you offer your help?

You watch them carry the bags and after they have put them where they wanted, you say:

“You should’ve asked for help!” You see them carrying the bags and you say:

“Do you want any help?” As soon as you see them you immediately rush to their side and say: “Please, allow me to take the bags for you.” How kind does the third one sound? If we try to be polite we will develop good manners.

Our Beloved Messenger Muḥammad said: “I promise a palace in the highest stages of Jannah for the person who has good manners.” (Abū Dāwūd) In another ḥadīth our Beloved Messenger Muḥammad told us that, in Paradise there are (special) palaces, which Allāh has prepared for those who feed the hungry, speak softly and gently, fast continuously and pray at night whilst people are asleep. It is said about our Beloved Messenger Muḥammad that he did not speak too slowly or too fast. He spoke in a medium tone. His words were neither too short nor too many but were always just right. When he spoke, people felt as if exactly the right amount of words were used. He was perfect in everything.

Next time you want to speak, STOP AND THINK!

“How can I be gentle in my speech?”
