Naw\ﬁl

Ṣalāh Allāh has made the ﬁve daily ṣalāh compulsory upon us. Ṣalāh can be thought of as 'meetings and conversations' with the King of the universe. It is a time when we get a chance to talk to our Creator and Provider.

Ṣalāh is a chance to seek the mercy and help of Allāh . Ṣalāh is a chance to become a friend of the Ruler of the universe. The Beloved Messenger had the most love for Allāh . He showed and taught us how to get even closer to Allāh and how to be Allāh 's closest friends. From among the ways to become much closer to Allāh is by performing extra ṣalāh. The Messenger of Allāh said, "Allāh, the Exalted, has said: 'I will declare war against he who harms my pious worshippers. And the most beloved thing by which My slave comes nearer to Me, is what I have made compulsory upon him; and My slave keeps on coming closer to Me by performing Naw\ﬁl." (Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī)

Ishrāq Ṣalātul Ishrāq is the name of a naﬂ ṣalāh that the Prophet would perform after sunrise.

Ṣalātul Ishrāq is best performed ﬁfteen minutes after sunrise, but it is acceptable to perform it anytime between then and just before midday.

One can perform two rak'āt or four rak'āt for Ishrāq. The Prophet said, "Whosoever performs the Morning Prayer (Fajr) with jamā'ah then remains seated and makes dhikr until sunrise, thereafter he performs two rak'āt, he will gain a reward similar to performing Ḥajj and 'Umrah." Our Beloved Messenger said, "A complete (Ḥajj and 'Umrah), complete, complete!" (Tirmidhī)

Ḍu#\ Another ṣalāh which the our Beloved Messenger Muḥammad would perform is called Ḍu#\. This would be performed in the second half of the morning, towards midday.

Nu'aym ibn Hammar said: "I heard the Messenger say: 'Allāh, the Exalted, says:

"O Son of Ādam, do not be weak in performing four rak'āt for Me at the beginning of the day: I will supply what you need till the end of the day."'"

(Abū Dāwūd) The minimum number of rak'āt of Ḍu#\ is 2 and the maximum is 12.

Tahajjud Tahajjud is the ṣalāh performed at night after waking up from one's sleep.

Tahajjud is the most rewarding naﬂ ṣalāh. The Prophet said regarding Tahajjud:

"Hold fast to the night prayer, indeed it is the trait of the pious predecessors, it is a means of nearness to your Lord, and it is an expiation for sins and a barrier from sins." (Tirmidhī) The best time to perform tahajjud is in the last portion of the night. However, if one feels that they will be unable to wake up at that time, then they may perform tahajjud after 'ishā' before witr ṣalāh. When half of the night or two thirds are over then Allāh calls out:

"Is there anyone asking, so that he be given? Is there anyone to make du'ā' so that he be answered? Is there any beggar of forgiveness so that he be forgiven? (And Allāh continues saying it) until the beginning of day (Fajr time)."

(Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim)

Taḥiyyatul Wuḍū'

'Taḥiyyatul Wuḍū'' refers to the two rak'āt a person is encouraged to perform after doing wuḍū'.

Abū Hurayrah narrates that the Messenger said to Bilāl : "O Bilāl, Tell me of the best deed you did after embracing Islam, for I heard your footsteps in front of me in Paradise." Bilāl replied, "I did not do anything worth mentioning except that whenever I performed wuḍū' during the day or night that I prayed after that wuḍū' as much ṣalāh as was written for me."

(Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī)

Taḥiyyatul Masjid 'Taḥiyyatul Masjid' refers to the two rak'āt which should be performed upon entering a masjid. This is offered as a means of expressing respect when entering the house of Allāh . The Beloved Messenger Muḥammad said, "Anyone performs wuḍū' and performs it perfectly and then offers two rak'ahs of prayers with devotion, then (entry into) Paradise becomes assured." (Abū Dāwūd)

One should offer two rak'āt whenever entering the masjid unless it is a time when it is makrūh to perform ṣalāh.

Breaking the ṣalāh It is compulsory to break one's ṣalāh even if it is a far} ṣalāh if somebody nearby is in desperate need or calls for urgent help. For example, if a person nearby has an epileptic ﬁt, a heart attack or something just as serious, one must break his ṣalāh and help that person. If a person is stealing your belongings or somebody else's belongings whilst you are in ṣalāh, it is wājib to break your ṣalāh and retrieve your belongings.

Khushū'

Another important point to remember with regard to ṣalāh is Khushū'.

Khushū' is humility and devotion in ṣalāh.

Allāh states in the Qur'ān when mentioning the traits of those that shall enter Jannah al-Firdaws.

"Success is really attained by the believers. Who concentrate their attention in humbleness when offering ṣalāh ….Those are the inheritors. Who will inherit Firdaws (the Paradise). They will be there forever."

(Qur'ān 23:1-11)

There are many ways a person can attain Khushū' in ṣalāh. A few are mentioned below:

  • Prepare yourself properly for ṣalāh.
  • Pray ṣalāh at a measured pace.
  • Remember Allāh throughout your ṣalāh and the

moments when you will stand before him on the day of Judgement.

  • Pause at the end of each verse.
  • Recite the verses in a slow and calm manner.
  • Vary the sūrahs that are recited.
  • Know that Allāh is listening to you and

responding to your words.

Some of the things that should be avoided in order to develop Khushū':

  • Do not pray ṣalāh when a meal is ready (unless

ṣalāh time will lapse) as then the focus in your ṣalāh will be the food that is waiting.

  • Do not pray ṣalāh when there is a need for

answering the call of nature.

  • Do not pray ṣalāh with clothes that may distract

yourself and others that are praying.

  • Do not pray ṣalāh in a lazy manner.
  • Do not ﬁdget.

Jamā'ah Ṣalāh

Ṣalāh with Jamā'ah is of great importance. To perform ṣalāh with jamā'ah is greatly emphasised for men. A person that leaves ṣalāh with jamā'ah without a valid reason will be sinful. The Prophet said: "A man's ṣalāh in congregation is twenty-ﬁve times more rewarding than his ṣalāh at home or in his shop, and that is because when he performs his wuḍū' properly and proceeds towards the masjid with the purpose of performing ṣalāh in congregation, he does not take a step without being raised a degree (in rank) for it and having a sin remitted for it, till he enters the masjid. When he is performing ṣalāh, the angels continue to invoke the Blessings of Allāh on him as long as he is in his place of worship in a state of wuḍū'. They say: 'O Allāh! Have mercy on him! O Allāh! Forgive him.' He is deemed to be engaged in ṣalāh as long as he waits for it.''

(Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī)

'Abdullāh ibn Mas'ūd reported that the Beloved Prophet said: "It is more excellent for a woman to pray in her house than in her courtyard, and more excellent for her to pray in the inner part of her house than in that portion of the house where it is open."

(Abū Dāwūd)

"By Him in Whose Hand my life is, I sometimes thought of giving orders for ﬁrewood to be collected, then for proclaiming the adhān for ṣalāh. Then I would appoint an imām to lead ṣalāh, and then go to the houses of those who do not come to perform ṣalāh in congregation, and set ﬁre to these houses.'' (Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī) It is very important to make sure the rows are straight. The Prophet would remind people before ṣalāh to straighten the rows. He would check himself if the rows were straight. The Prophet said, "Make the rows straight and do not differ, lest your hearts differ."

(Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim)

Nikāḥ

Nikāḥ (marriage) is a great sunnah in Islām. When a man and a woman marry, love, peace and companionship is created between them. The Prophet equated marriage to half of one's faith. He said: "Whoever marries has completed (by protecting) half of his faith: let him fear violating the other half." (Ṭabarān|)

The purpose of marriage A Muslim should marry because:

1)Marriage is a form of worshipping Allāh .

2)It is the sunnah (way) of the Prophets .

3)A human being naturally needs to marry.

4)Marriage is a means of having children.

5)Marriage is a source of happiness.

6)Marriage will protect you from many sins.

7)Marriage gives you a partner to protect you and help you.

What qualities to look for in a spouse? The Prophet said: "Women are (usually) married for four reasons: for their wealth, ancestry, beauty and piety. Marry the pious one and you will be successful." (Ṣahīh al-Bukhārī) From all the different qualities in a person, piety is the most desirable quality. A pious spouse will respect you, listen to you, help you, fear Allāh when in a disagreement with you, will not oppress you and will try their best to fulﬁl your rights. A pious person will try their best to cultivate good character. This is because they fear the displeasure of Allāh . That same person will be truthful, honest and trustworthy. These are the greatest qualities you need in a husband or wife.

Beauty and handsomeness should also be thought of when accepting a proposal. It is important one is attracted to one's spouse to help protect one's gaze.

Ad'iyah (supplications) for ﬁnding the right spouse "Our Lord, give us good in this world and good in the Hereafter, and save us from the punishment of Fire." (Qur'ān 2:201)

"O My Lord! I am in need of whatever good you send down to me." (Qur'ān 28:24)

"Our Lord, grant us from our spouses and children comfort to our eyes and make us a leader for the righteous." (Qur'ān 25:74)

Before Marriage It is ḥarām (totally impermissible) to have any relationship before marriage. Even after a couple are engaged, it is not permissible for them to have contact with one another. Before marrying a person, it is permissible to see them. This should be done in the most modest and appropriate manner.

Who can't I marry?

There are certain people one cannot marry. They are known as maḥram (plural ma#ārim). They consist of the following:

Forbidden forever due to blood relations:

  • Parents, grandparents
  • Children, grandchildren
  • Brothers, sisters
  • Father's brothers or sisters
  • Mother's brothers or sisters
  • Nephews and nieces

Permanently forbidden due to marriage

  • Parents of spouse
  • Spouse of one's children
  • Step-parents
  • Step-children if your marriage is consummated

Permanently forbidden due to foster relations

  • A woman who breastfed you before the age of two.
  • The husband of the woman who breastfed you

before the age of two.

  • Anybody who was breastfed by the same woman

before the age of two.

  • Any person which your wife breastfed before the age of

two.

  • Those who are forbidden due to blood relations will be

forbidden through foster relations too.

Offer and Acceptance + Witnesses = Nikāḥ A nikāḥ is simply done with a valid offer and acceptance in the presence of witnesses. The offer is known as |jāb. The acceptance is known as qabūl. The |jāb and qabūl must be verbal, said in the past or present tense and must take place in the same gathering.

There must be at least two male witnesses or one male and two female witnesses who are:

1)Muslim 2) Mature 3) Sane If the above is found, nikāḥ will take place.

Note: A person may be represented if they are unable to attend the gathering of nika# in-person. This representation is known as wakālah.

Mahr (Dower) When getting married, it is necessary for the groom to give mahr to the bride.

Mahr is wājib so that the husband appreciates his wife. It is not necessary to give the mahr at the time of nikāḥ. If there is mutual agreement, the mahr can even be deferred until after the marriage ceremony.

Anything of monetary value can be given as mahr. It is not necessary to give cash only.

However, the amount of mahr must be mutually agreed between the bride and groom. The mahr is solely the property of the wife. She may do with it as she pleases.

Different types of Mahr Although one may give any amount above the minimum, there are certain famous amounts of mahr.

Minimum Mahr The minimum amount of mahr is the value of 10 dirhams (30.618 grams of silver).

Mahr azwāj an-Nab| The Prophet commonly gave 500 dirhams (1530.9 grams of silver).

Mahr F\ṭim| This is the amount of mahr given by 'Al| to Fāṭimah upon their marriage.

Although there is a difference of opinion on the precise amount, many scholars state that this was 480 dirhams (1469.66 grams of silver).

The recommended method of nikāḥ 1)To have the nikāḥ ceremony on a Friday.

2)To have the nikāḥ ceremony in the masjid.

3)Scholars and the pious should be invited.

4)Have the nikāḥ performed by a pious person.

5)To give a feast after the nikāḥ is consummated is sunnah. The feast is called 'walīmah'.

Ṭalāq

FiqhFiqh Everybody has ups and downs. Brothers and sisters have good days and bad days together. Likewise, a husband and wife may experience some good days and some bad days.

Anytime you experience anger in your marriage, extinguish the anger by:

  • Drinking a glass of water
  • Changing your position to a lower state: If you

are standing, sit down. If you are sitting, lie down.

  • Recite Ta'awwudh

FiqhFiqh The Qur'ān has given us clear guidance on how to resolve a difference.

1)Discuss the fear of Allāh and accountability in the court of Allāh for wrongdoings.

2)If the above does not solve the problem, temporarily separate from each other. Give each other space to cool down and think things over. Sleep in separate rooms and give each other time.

3)Relatives or senior people known to both husband and wife could be called upon to help resolve the differences.

If all avenues and efforts to resolve the dispute between husband and wife have been exhausted, yet irreconcilable differences remain, Islām has given a way to terminate the marriage. This is known as ṭalāq (divorce).

Different types of Ṭalāq There are two types of Ṭalāq:

1)Ṭalāq Raj'| (revocable divorce)

2)Ṭalāq Bā'in (irrevocable divorce)

Ṭalāq Raj'| (Revocable divorce) This refers to a ṭalāq where a husband and wife can get back together later on.

Ṭalāq raj'| will take place when the husband says:"I give you ṭalāq." If the husband says it once, one ṭalāq raj'| will occur. If the husband says it twice, two ṭalāq raj'| will occur.

Once a husband gives one ṭalāq, he will only have two left. If he were to give two ṭalāqs, he would only have one left. If he gives three ṭalāqs, he will not be allowed to return to his wife thereafter. If by chance she gets married to another person and then gets divorced, she may re-marry her ﬁrst husband after the waiting period (iddah) is over.

After a ṭalāq raj'|, if the 'iddah (waiting period) has not ended then a new marriage is not required to get back together. Simply making up and getting back together is sufﬁcient within the 'iddah.

Ṭalāq will occur when a person says to his wife that he is giving her ṭalāq. If he says this when angry, then ṭalāq will still take place. The intention does not make a difference either. If a man were to say "I give you ṭalāq" to his wife thinking simply to scare or threaten her, then ṭalāq would still take place. Even by writing "I divorce my wife," ṭalāq will take place. Ṭalāq will occur regardless of it being written or spoken.

Different number of Ṭalāq If a husband says to his wife "I give you ṭalāq," this will result in one ṭalāq raj'|. If a husband says, "I give you ṭalāq, ṭalāq," This will be regarded as two ṭalāq raj'|.

Ṭalāq Bā'in (Irrevocable divorce) This refers to a ṭalāq where-after a person will have to perform a new nikāḥ with his wife in order to be with her again.

Ṭalāq Bā'in will take place when a husband intends to give ṭalāq with words which have a meaning of ṭalāq or are used to give ṭalāq. For example, if the husband says to the wife, "It's over," intending to give ṭalāq, one ṭalāq bā'in will take place. If the husband says it twice, two Ṭalāq Bā'in will take place. If he gives three Ṭalāq Bā'in, he will not be allowed to return thereafter. If by chance she gets married to another person and then gets divorced, she may re-marry her ﬁrst husband after the waiting period (iddah) is over.

'Iddah (Waiting period) A woman has been ordained to observe 'iddah after a divorce or upon the demise of her husband. In the waiting period, a woman is not permitted to marry anyone besides the husband who gave her ṭalāq bā'in. After the 'iddah is complete, she is free to marry whomever she wishes. A woman must abstain from the following during 'iddah:

1)Going out of the house without necessity.

2)Wearing cosmetics.

3)Dressing up in an appealing manner. The duration of the 'iddah is three menstrual cycles. If a woman is not menstruating, then she will observe 'iddah for 90 days (3 months). The 'iddah of pregnant woman is until she gives birth. In the scenario of the husband passing away, the wife will have to observe 'iddah for 4 months and 10 days (130 days). The woman will spend the 'iddah at her house and the husband will be responsible for all her needs during the 'iddah.

Buyū'

"The truthful merchant is rewarded by being ranked on the Day of Resurrection with the prophets, the truthful elite and the martyrs." (Tirmidhī)

There are some conditions which must be present at the time of buying and selling items:

1)The product must be ḥalāl. It is not permissible to sell ḥarām items. For example, it is not permissible to sell alcohol, stolen goods, pork etc.

2)The price and the item for sale must be clearly speciﬁed at the time of the transaction.

3)There should not be any ambiguity in any aspect of the transaction. For example, at the time of selling a mobile phone, the make, model, condition and colour should all be clearly advertised and described to a customer.

4)The transaction should be unconditional. For example, Mūsā sells a watch to Idrīs on the condition that Idrīs gives Mūsā the watch to wear once a week. This will not be permissible.

Ijārah

"O you who believe, when you transact a debt payable at a speciﬁed time, put it in writing..."

(Qur'ān 2:282)

Allāh teaches us to write things down when we conduct any transaction to avoid any confusion and misunderstanding between the two parties.

Remember the Arabic proverb:

"Live like brothers and deal like strangers."

Ijārah refers to employment and leasing.

'Ā'ishah reports that the Prophet said:

"Verily Allāh loves perfection in one's work."

(Abū Ya'l\)

Keeping the above ḥadīth in mind, when you work for somebody, do your best to fulﬁl your duties in the best possible manner.

Before taking any job or employing someone, the following must be clearly agreed:

1)Job description 2)Hours and days of work 3)Contract length 4)Wages 5)Date of payment of wages 6)Holidays Ijārah also refers to renting. When renting or hiring anything, it is important that the following is clear:

1)The item which will be rented.

2)The rent a person has to pay.

3)The duration for which rent is payable.

4)The contract length.

Ribā

Ribā means interest.

Interest is charging money for giving someone a loan.

ĪFor example, if Yūsuf wanted a loan of 10,000, the bank only gives it to him on condition that Yūsuf ĪĪpays back 12,000. The extra 2,000 is interest and is ḥarām to give and take. Through interest, the rich become richer and the poor become poorer.

Allāh states in the Qur'ān:

"Those who consume interest shall not stand up on the Day of Resurrection except like him who Shayṭān has driven mad by his touch. That is because they say: "Trade is just like interest." But Allāh has permitted trade and has forbidden interest. So whoever has received an admonition from his Lord and desists may have that (amount) which has passed, and his affair rests with Allāh. But whoever returns to dealing in interest - they are the companions of the Fire; they will abide eternally therein." (Qur'ān 2:275)

In another verse, Allāh states:

"Allāh destroys interest and increases charity. And Allāh does not like every sinning disbeliever."

(Qur'ān 2:276) A question arises that by giving charity one's wealth decreases and by receiving interest, one's wealth Īseems to increase. For example, Zayd had 100, ĪĪhe gave 20 in charity, so he is now left with 80.

ĪĪĀdam also had a 100, he received 20 in interest, Īso he now has a balance of 120. So then what does this verse mean?

What is meant by 'destroys interest' is that Allāh deprives the money generated by interest of all blessings. Despite owning hundreds and thousands, his needs will not be fulﬁlled. He will be forced to pay where he doesn't desire to do so. On top of that, the calamities of this world will be let loose upon him. Most terrifying of all is the punishment that awaits him in the Hereafter.

With regards to charity, Allāh showers His blessing on the one who spends in charity. Thus with little provision and means, Allāh will fulﬁl all his needs. In addition to that, Allāh will avert from him the calamities of this world. Finally, and most importantly, the charity will be a means of salvation in the Hereafter. In another verse, Allāh announces, "O you, who have believed, fear Allāh and give up what remains due to you of interest, if you are believers. And if you do not, then be warned of war against you from Allāh and His Messenger. But if you repent, you may have your principal amount - thus oppress not and you shall not be oppressed."

(Qur'ān 2:278-279)

Jābir narrates that the Beloved Messenger Muḥammad said, "Cursed is the receiver of interest, the payer of interest, the one who records it and the two witnesses to the transaction and said: "They are all alike in guilt." (Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim)

Therefore, dealing in interest, charging interest, giving interest: are all Ḥarām.

Gambling

"O you who believe! Eat of the lawful things that We have provided you." (Qur'ān 2:172)

"O those who believe, verily wine, gambling, altar- stones and divining arrows are ﬁlth, a work of Shayṭān.

Therefore, refrain from it, so that you may be successful. The Shayṭān wants nothing but to plant enmity and malice between you through wine and gambling, and to prevent you from the remembrance of Allāh and from the ṣalāh. Would you, then, abstain?" (Qur'ān 5:90-91)

Gambling, betting, lotteries and similar activities are all ḥarām. Many people become addicted to such activities which in turn causes great harm in this world as well as the hereafter. Many people have become bankrupt through an addiction to gambling.

Any money gained through such activities will be classed as ḥarām and hence the food purchased with such money will also be ḥarām. Our Beloved Messenger Muḥammad mentioned a man who, having journeyed far, is disheveled and dusty, who spreads out his hands to the sky saying “O Lord!

O Lord!,” while his food is ḥarām, his drink is ḥarām, his clothing is ḥarām, and he has been nourished with ḥarām, so how can his supplication be answered?

(Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim)

The Different Schools of Fiqh Fiqh is the deduction and codiﬁcation of the laws of the Qur'ān and a#ādīth. A person who follows rulings mentioned in the books of Fiqh is in reality following the Qur'ān and a#ādīth as those very rulings have been juristically deduced from the Qur'ān and a#ādīth. The scholars who codiﬁed Fiqh were known as Mujtahidūn. They were expert jurists who had mastered all the various sciences related to the Arabic language, Qur'ān, Aḥādīth and Fiqh.

There were many such scholars in the ﬁrst three hundred years of Islām. They codiﬁed the laws of the Qur'ān and Aḥādīth to the best of their ability.

Each mujtahid (master jurist) exercised his insight, understanding and juristic skills to deduce various laws from the Qur'ān and Aḥādīth. This obviously resulted in differences from jurist to jurist.

The Different Schools of Fiqh The four most famous jurists were:

1)Imām Abū Ḥanīfah (80AH-150AH)

Imām Abū Ḥanīfah was born in 80 AH in Kūfa, Iraq. He was a Tābi'| (someone who has met a Ṣa#āb|) as he had met Anas . He was a merchant and thereafter devoted his time in the pursuit of knowledge. His main teacher was Ḥammād . He studied under him for nearly two decades. After the demise of his teacher, Imām Abū Ḥanīfah was appointed as the head lecturer. For approximately thirty years, he lectured and had great scholars under his tutelage with whom he established the Ḥanaf| school of Fiqh. His most famous students were Imām Abū Yūsuf and Imām Muḥammad .

The Different Schools of Fiqh 2)Imām Mālik ibn Anas (93AH-179AH)

Imām Mālik was born in Madīnah and lived there for his entire life. At a very young age, he memorised the Qur'ān and studied under the great scholar Imām N\ﬁ' . He also studied under another great scholar Ibn Hurmuz for some time.

Imām Mālik began to lecture and teach in Masjid an-Nabaw| only after seventy scholars of Madīnah testiﬁed to his capability. He was extremely respectful of the ḥadīth of our Beloved Messenger . He would apply perfume, wear nice clothing and sit humbly in his place out of respect for the words of our Beloved Messenger Muḥammad . His most famous work was al-Muwa{ṭa', one of the earliest compilations of Ḥadīth. After his demise, he was buried in the famous graveyard in Madīnah, Jannatul Baq|'.

The way in which 'Uthmān 's good character became apparent was through the company of our Beloved Messenger . 'Uthmān was trained in accordance to the Qur'ānic method by his teacher; Muḥammad . He was also taught the Sunnah, the rules of tilāwah and the way in which souls are cleansed (Tazkiyah).

'Uthmān and the Battle of Badr Once our Beloved Messenger had established the foundations in Madīnah, he then moved onto creating chains of brotherhood between the Anār and Muhājirūn. 'Uthmān who was a Muhājir created a bond with his 'brother'

Aws ibn Thābit. The Messenger Muḥammad then went onto stabilising his masjid, sending out expeditions, making a treaty with the Jewish tribes, and focussing his attention on laying down the ﬁnancial and educational fundaments of the new society. 'Uthmān was one of the key ﬁgures and he did not hold back any suggestions, judgments or wealth. He was present at every major event apart from the Battle of Badr as he had to nurse his wife Ruqayyah in her sickness.

Ruqayyah passed away reciting the Shahādah, however, she did not get to say her ﬁnal farewell to her beloved father; Muḥammad as he was at the Battle of Badr with his Ṣa#ābah, determined to make the word of Allāh absolute, thus he could not attend her burial.

The Campaigns The Campaign of Ghaṭafān Our Beloved Messenger prepared the Muslims and then set off with four hundred men and a number of horses. He left 'Uthmān in charge of Madīnah. A polytheist member of the Ban[ Tha'laba tribe, by the name of Jābir was caught at Dhul Qaṣah. He was then taken to the Messenger and told him information about his people: "They will never meet you in battle: when they heard you were coming they ﬂed to the mountain tops. I will go with you." Hearing this, our Beloved Messenger hastened in inviting him to Islām, and Jābir accepted. For the remainder of the eleven day excursion from Madīnah, there were no further encounters with any enemies, and therefore no further hostilities.

Campaign of Dhāt ar-riqā' The Beloved Messenger came to learn that the two tribes Tha'laba and Anmār were going to attack Madīnah. He therefore decided to set off with four hundred men. He appointed 'Uthmān in charge of Madīnah once again. The Muslims came across a large group of people at Ghaṭafan but no ﬁghting was witnessed. This time he was absent from Madīnah for ﬁfteen days.

Bay'ah ar-Riḍwān When the Muslims were on their way to perform Umrah, they were stopped from entering into Makkah, so they stopped at Hudaybiyyah. Kharash Ibn Umayyah Al Khuzā'| was then elected to represent the Muslims. So he went into Makkah by camel.

The Quraysh killed the camel as soon as he passed the city walls, and they also wanted to kill Kharash but the A#ābīsh stopped them. Kharash then returned to the Messenger and told him what had happened, so the Messenger elected a second representative:

'Umar . However 'Umar suggested that 'Uthmān would be the preferred nominee since he had the added advantage of tribal protection. And so it was that 'Uthmān went into Makkah to inform the Quraysh that the Muslims were not here to ﬁght but to perform 'Umrah. As he was approaching Makkah he was stopped by some Quraysh at a place called Baldah. They asked him where he was going, and 'Uthmān replied that the Messenger had sent him to speak to the Quraysh.

'Uthmān then entered Makkah and went to each leader. A suggestion was made that 'Uthmān should carry out 'Umrah by himself but he refused. As he had been gone a while, a rumour spread amongst the Muslims that 'Uthmān had been killed. This angered the Muslims greatly and they began planning revenge. The Messenger called his Ṣa#ābah and made them swear allegiance, pledging to ﬁght the Quraysh, and this oath was made under a tree. This pledge is known as Bay'ah ar-Riḍwān.

'Uthmān 's generosity in the Campaign of Tabūk In the year 9 AH, Heraclius began planning an attack on Arabia. The Muslims prepared for battle, but they lacked resources and equipment.

'Uthmān was known to be a very generous man, and he supplied the army with 940 camels and 60 horses; he also put forward 10,000 dinars in order to equip the army. 'Uthmān was the one who spent the most on the campaign. He did not take anything back.

'Uthmān at the time of Abū Bakr 'Uthmān was one of the Ahl ash-Shūr\, those whose views were required regarding the most solemn issues during the khilāfah of Abū Bakr . He was very close to Abū Bakr , to the point where he was the keeper of secrets and a senior scribe. His views held great sway in the court of Abū Bakr , for when Abū Bakr was in the midst of a tough decision he would always turn to 'Uthmān and act according with what he had to say. On one occasion when Abū Bakr became very ill, he asked the people who should take his place as the next khalīfah. They all suggested 'Umar . Then Abū Bakr turned to ask 'Uthmān , who was in full agreement.

During Abū Bakr 's Khilāfah, there was a drought, so the Amīr al-Mu'minīn told his people to have ṣabr as Allāh the Most Merciful would grant them food by evening. The workers of 'Uthmān had just returned from Syria, bringing him one hundred camels laden with wheat. The people gathered at 'Uthmān 's door. 'Uthmān gave all the food as charity for the poor Muslims.

During the Khilāfah of 'Umar People knew that 'Umar admired 'Uthmān deeply, to the extent that when they thought to ask 'Umar about some matter they would ﬁrst go to 'Uthmān and 'Abdur Raḥmān ibn 'Awf .

'Uthmān was known as 'ar-Radīf': the word 'Radīf' in Arabic means the one who rides behind another man and can play his part. The people would use this title to refer to a man who would one day, inevitably, be a leader. When 'Umar became a khalīfah, he discussed with the Ṣa#ābah his salary from the Bayt al-Māl (the Muslim Treasury) and 'Uthmān 's opinion was: "Eat and feed others". During the Khilāfah of 'Umar , 'Uthmān was known as the advisor, just as during Abū Bakr 's Khilāfah 'Umar was his advisor.

The Keeping of Records (Dīwān)

Once the conquests had advanced, and the Muslim Empire started to grow, there was the issue of the accumulation of wealth. 'Uthmān worried that there was no proper method of accounting.

'Umar readily agreed to this, and so they set up the dīwān. The Hijr| Calendar 'Uthmān was one of the Companions who suggested that the Islamic calendar should commence with the hijrah. The hijrah was a pivotal point in the history of Islām so it was only fair that the Islamic calendar begins from this great event.

'Umar appoints 'Uthmān as his successor 'Umar 's concern always remained as the well- being and on the solidarity of the Ummah. This was even the case when he was on his death bed. He was conscious that our Beloved Messenger passed away without selecting a successor, and he was well aware that Abū bakr had consulted with the senior Ṣa#ābah before he had passed away. However, when 'Umar was experiencing his last moments, he thought of a different method for selecting the next Khalīfah. His chosen method was known as 'Shūrā', whereby a number of men of varying credentials would be gathered together to make a decision by consensus. On this occasion 'Umar selected six; 'Al| ibn Ab| Ṭālib, 'Uthmān ibn 'Affān, 'Abdur Raḥmān ibn 'Awf, Sa'd ibn Ab| Waqqās, Zubayr ibn al-'Awwām and Ṭalḥah ibn Ubaydullāh . He took the precautionary measure of not inviting a family member, so that he could at no time be accused of favouritism.

'Umar told the six nominees to gather in one of their houses and carry out a consultation amongst themselves. This consultation lasted for three days. 'Abdullāh ibn 'Umar was present amongst them but only as an advisor. Two people Sīrahwere selected to watch over them, and another was selected to lead the prayers.

'Umar said that if there was some disagreement with regards to the ﬁnal decision between a few of the nominees then they would be referred to Abdur Raḥmān ibn 'Awf , as he was wise and a man of excellent counsel. After much deliberation, it was ﬁnally decided that 'Uthmān ibn Affān would be selected as the third khalīfah of Islām.

The advice that was given to 'Uthmān the as next Khalīfah:

• To fear Allāh in secrecy and in public, in both word Sīrah and action.

• To deal with relatives and strangers alike without any favouritism.

• Observing justice would be the best means of establishing power and respect as well as introducing social and political stability.

• To take care of the ﬁrst Muslims from amongst the Muhājirūn and Anār, as a way of acknowledging their high status in Islām, due to their striving.

• To guarantee that wealth is given out in a fair and proper way amongst all the inhabitants of the Empire.

• To avoid over-burdening Ahl Adh-Dhimmah (the Jews and Christians living in Muslims lands under the government's protection).

• Make sure that people's ﬁnancial rights are protected.

• Ensure that people of all races and tribes are protected, their needs met and their rights are not compromised or neglected.

• Abstain from egoism and corruption.

• Respect the young and old.

• Listen to people with a sympathetic ear, and without prejudice.

• Uphold the truth and inculcate it at all levels of society.

'Al|

'Al| was the son of Abū Ṭālib. Abū Ṭālib was the famous uncle and aide of the Prophet .

Thus, 'Al| was the cousin of the Beloved Prophet .

'Al| was born ten years before the Beloved Prophet received Prophethood.

Abū Ṭālib was struggling ﬁnancially and a famine had hit Makkah. The Prophet wanted to help his uncle. Therefore, he offered to help by raising one of the children of Abū Ṭālib. The Prophet took 'Al| under his roof and cared for him from a very young age.

Accepting Islām When the Prophet ﬁrst received revelation, 'Al| was ten years old. In the very ﬁrst couple of days, 'Al| saw the Beloved Prophet and Khadījah worshipping Allāh . He was curious and asked what they were doing. The Beloved Prophet told him about Islām and the revelation he had received. Not long after, 'Al| accepted Islām.

'Al| was the ﬁrst child to accept Islām.

Life in Makkah For thirteen years in Makkah, 'Al| lived with the Prophet . He was fortunate to sit, learn and observe the Beloved Messenger very closely every day.

'Al| would accompany the Prophet at times when he would call the people of Makkah to Islām. The night of Hijrah When the persecution of the Muslims was intense in Makkah, the Prophet gave permission to the Muslims to migrate to Madīnah.

Whilst most of the Muslims took this permission on board and migrated, Abū Bakr and 'Al| remained behind with the Messenger 's consent. The people of Quraysh planned to assassinate the Beloved Prophet . The night the Quraysh surrounded his house was the same night Allāh instructed him to leave and migrate to Madīnah.

The Prophet instructed 'Al| to stay in his house and particularly requested that he sleep in his bed the very same night. He had guaranteed 'Al| that Allāh would protect him and no harm would come to him.

Thus, 'Al| slept the entire night peacefully in the bed of the Prophet whilst the house of the Prophet was surrounded by the Quraysh. The Quraysh were furious when they realised the Prophet had slipped from their grasp. For a couple of days, 'Al| stayed in Makkah and distributed the belongings of Quraysh which had been entrusted to the Prophet . Thereafter, 'Al| also left for Madīnah.

Building Masjid an-Nabaw| 'Al| enthusiastically took part in building the historic Masjid in Madīnah. He would pick up heavy bricks and mud and transport them. We cannot underestimate the immense legacy of the help that the Ṣa#ābah so willingly gave.

Battle of Badr The ﬁrst and greatest battle in the history of Islām, Badr, took place in the second year after the migration to Madīnah.

'Al| was among the fortunate ones to take part in this great victory for Islām. In the very beginning, the Quraysh asked the Muslims to duel. 'Al| was among three Ṣa#ābah chosen to duel in the battle of Badr. He was very strong and excellent at duelling. His opponent was Walīd. 'Al| ﬁnished Walīd off with one strike. Upon seeing 'Al| triumph and succeed, all the Muslims shouted, "Allāhu Akbar!"

'Al| not only took part, he was a strong example of a soldier who fought with much courage and bravery.

Nikāḥ In the second year of Hijrah, 'Al| proposed to Fāṭimah , the beloved daughter of the Sīrah Prophet . The Prophet accepted the proposal and asked 'Al| to provide mahr payment. 'Al| went and sold some of his belongings, returning to the Prophet with 480 Dirhams as mahr. The walīmah was extremely frugal. It consisted of the most basic of meals in the simplest of fashions.

Battle of Uḥud 'Al| stayed with the Prophet during the difﬁculties in the battle of Uḥud. He displayed his bravery and courage whilst being surrounded by the enemies.

'Al| was among those Ṣa#ābah who helped the Prophet climb the mountain of Uḥud.

The conquest of Khaybar Khaybar was the stronghold of the Jews. It was a township to the north of Madīnah that was full of strong forts. The Beloved Messenger selected Abū Bakr as the leader of the army.

Thereafter he selected 'Umar as the leader.

However, the forts were still not conquered.

Thus the Prophet said, "I will give the ﬂag to such a person tomorrow that Allāh and His Messenger loves and he loves Allāh and His Messenger. Allāh will grant conquest at his hands." The Ṣa#ābah became very curious: who was going to have this great honour? Every Ṣa#āb| had the same sincere desire and feeling: to be able to receive the ﬂag from the Messenger knowing Allāh and His Messenger loved him.

Everybody spent the night with hope and desire. In the morning, their curiosity and excitement increased. Later, 'Umar expressed his excitement and sincere desire by saying, "I had never wanted to be the commander so much as I wanted it at the time of Khaybar."

All of the Ṣahābah were waiting with the same intense anticipation. The Beloved Messenger ordered the ﬂag to be fetched after the morning prayer. The ﬂag was brought. Everybody was looking at the ﬂag in the blessed hand of the Prophet .

Amongst this curiosity and excitement, the Beloved Messenger asked, "Where is 'Al|?"

'Al| had pain in his eyes at that time. They said, "O Messenger of Allāh! He has pain in his eyes." The Messenger said, "Call him here." When 'Al| was informed about it, he duly approached the Prophet . The pain in his eyes was cured through the blessed prayer of the Messenger of Allāh . With the ﬂag of the Prophet in his hand, Allāh granted victory to the Muslims under the leadership of 'Al| .

The Ḥajj message In the ninth year of hijrah, the Prophet sent Abū Bakr with a group of Muslims to perform Ḥajj. Sūrah Tawbah was revealed at the time, with a message for the Makkan idol worshippers. The Prophet ordered 'Al| to go and deliver this message to everybody in Makkah during the days of Ḥajj. The journey to Yemen In the tenth year of hijrah, the Prophet sent 'Al| to Yemen to give da'wah and spread the message of Islām.

'Al| joined the Prophet in Ḥajj on his return from Yemen.

The passing away of the Beloved Prophet When the Prophet departed from this Sīrah world, 'Al| took the foremost role in the whole janāzah by virtue of being a relative of the Beloved Prophet . He washed and shrouded the body of the Beloved Messenger . The Khilāfah of Abū Bakr Abū Bakr was selected as the amīr and leader of the Muslims after the passing away of the Prophet .

Fāṭimah was extremely upset after her father's demise. She was also ill. During the months that followed, 'Al| looked after Fāṭimah and focused all his efforts on domestic chores. After six months, Fāṭimah left this world.

'Al| supported Abū Bakr during his khilāfah.

The Khilāfah of 'Umar and Sīrah'Uthmān During the Khilāfah of 'Umar and 'Uthmān , 'Al| offered his unconditional support and would be among those to give advice to them. When 'Uthmān was experiencing some unrest from the people, 'Al| tried his utmost to settle the unrest. He supported 'Uthmān with all that he had. When the house of 'Uthmān was under siege, 'Al| came to the people and tried his utmost to calm the people down.

Some people broke into the house of 'Uthmān and martyred him. When 'Al| found out, he was extremely distressed.

Becoming the Khalīfah After the martyrdom of 'Uthmān , the situation was such that there was no khalīfah for three days.

People began coming to 'Al| and urging him to become the next khalīfah. He rejected the people's call. When the Muhājirūn and Anār came in great numbers and requested 'Al| to become the khalīfah, he found it impossible to refuse.

One of the ﬁrst tasks 'Al| attended to was to avenge the martyrdom of 'Uthmān and bring the guilty to justice.

Due to the commotion and sheer number of people, the exact people who martyred 'Uthmān could not be identiﬁed. It was difﬁcult to pinpoint a murderer without concrete evidence.

Thereafter, 'Al| changed all the governors across the Islamic Empire as a great number of complaints had built up against the previous ones.

Some Ṣa#ābah wanted the Khalīfah to exhaust all efforts in avenging the martyrdom of 'Uthmān .

In Makkah Many people in Makkah were upset that the murderers of 'Uthmān weren't brought to justice. 'Ā'ishah , Zubayr and Ṭalḥah were all in Makkah.

Many people joined in the calls of seeking justice for the martyrdom of 'Uthmān . Some of them were from the family of 'Uthmān , the Ban[ Umayyah. A movement began. This movement demanded that the people be brought to justice. They decided to go to Irāq and secure funds, raising awareness and support for their movement. 'Al| heard about the movement and decided himself to go to Irāq to try and restore law and order.

'Al| told the people of Madīnah to come along to Iraq. Besides a few Ṣa#ābah, most people accompanied 'Al| .

In Irāq The people of Baṣrah had already sided with those demanding justice for 'Uthmān .

'Al| sent Ḥasan to Kufa to win support and help the Khalīfah. The people of Kufa accepted the invitation and rose up in arms to support 'Al| .

'Al| , along with the people of Kufa, decided to go to Baṣrah.

Baṣrah had three groups:

1)Supporters of 'Al| .

2)Supporters of 'Ā'ishah who was leading the calls for justice on behalf of 'Uthmān .

3)Neutral people, 'Al| and 'Ā'ishah did not want a confrontation, let alone further blood to spill.

Both wanted to calm the situation and talk things through. Before the evening, all thoughts of battle had gone and things were very positive. An agreement was almost made. They decided to conclude the agreement the next day.

The murderers of 'Uthmān were present. They were aware that if the agreement was allowed to consolidate, they would be exposed and taken to task. Likewise, there were some people with 'Ā'ishah who wanted revenge at all costs. At night time, the murderers of 'Uthmān attacked 'Ā'ishah 's camp causing confusion and havoc in the dark. Each group thought they had been attacked ﬁrst by the other. A full blown battle began to rage. 'Ā'ishah mounted her camel and raced to the battleﬁeld to try and calm the matter down and stop those in her camp.

'Al| tried his best to calm things down as he entered into the fray.

Some senior Ṣa#ābah left the battleﬁeld knowing that the whole situation was unjustiﬁed.

Many people passed away defending the camel of 'Ā'ishah .

'Al| saw that both parties would not stop the battle until the camel of 'Ā'ishah was felled down. He gestured to somebody to attack the camel. It was struck, and it duly fell to ground. Upon seeing the camel drop to the ground, those defending it ran and left their positions. The battle was all but over.

'Al| ordered that nobody should be pursued, no spoils of war should be taken and whoever had surrendered should be dealt with peacefully. This battle is famously remembered as:

'The Battle of the Camel.'

Transferring the capital city of the SīrahIslamic Empire After the battle, 'Al| visited Kufa. He decided to reside in Kufa. Thus the capital of the Islamic Empire moved from Madīnah to Kufa.

One of the reasons why 'Al| moved to Kufa was to protect and safeguard the sanctity of Madīnah. He did not want any repetition of what had happened to 'Uthmān .

Therefore, he moved the political hub away from the sacred city of Madīnah. The people of Kufa offered 'Al| an abode in a white palace they had. He refused in the same way that 'Umar had declined the excessive comforts offered to him.

Invitation to people to give bay'ah To unite everybody and bring calm to the Islamic Empire, 'Al| sent envoys to those who had not yet pledged allegiance to him to tell them to pledge allegiance to him as the rightful Khalīfah. He sent an envoy to Mu'āwiyah , but he refrained from giving bay'ah. He wanted those responsible for the martyrdom of 'Uthmān to be brought to justice.

Since Mu'āwiyah 's followers refused to give bay'ah, Al| decided to make a personal visit to the region of Shām, where they resided. Upon hearing of the impending visit, Mu'āwiyah prepared to march out to meet them.

The Battle of Ṣiffīn With the prospect of a confrontation between the people of Irāq and Shām, 'Al| ordered that none of the people with him should start any military engagement with the people of Shām.

Instead, they should keep explaining to them to give allegiance to 'Al| . He further instructed them that if Mu'āwiyah 's people didn't agree and instead rejected then no ﬁghting should take place unless initiated by the other side. When the two armies were facing each other, for a few days they were locked in a stand-off. Some of the people of Irāq tried to call the people of Shām to give bay'ah. The people of Irāq were thirsty and tried to access water in the area, but the people of Shām prevented them. This was the spark that caused ﬁghting to erupt.

Battle continued between the two armies for some time. Both armies were looking for a way to end the bloodshed and bring about a peaceful solution as both groups identiﬁed themselves as Muslims. Many ideas and opinions were raised in an attempt to end the battle. In the end, both parties agreed to have a man from each side negotiate a truce.

The Khawārij A group of people from 'Al| 's camp disliked this action and regarded such an action as kufr. These people were known as Khawārij. The Khawārij would kill any Muslim who disagreed with them and would declare Muslims disbelievers on small issues.

'Al| perceived the threat from the Khawārij and fought them.

Many of them ﬂed and the rest were killed at Nahrawān.

The personal qualities of 'Al| His trustworthiness 'Al| was extremely reliable and trustworthy. The Prophet entrusted him with the belongings of people at the time of hijrah. His humility He was really humble. He would be busy farming, tending to livestock and engaging in other chores and people would approach him to ask him questions.

Once the Prophet walked into the masjid and found 'Al| sleeping on sand in a very simple and humble manner. His justice After 'Al| became the leader, he was still humble to the law and authorities. Once, he had a court case with a Jewish person about the ownership of an armour. The judge ruled in favour of the Jewish person because 'Al| did not have sufﬁcient proof.

His helping people 'Al| would walk in the markets and help anyone that was lost. Likewise, he would help carry the load of the weak and old. His humbleness After becoming the leader, he would still go and buy food himself from the shops. One day, he went to the market and bought dates for one dirham. The importance of isnād (Chain of transmission)

One of the special features of this Ummah is the tradition of isnād.

Everything that has reached us has a chain of narrators. We know every person in that chain linking us with the Prophet . This is a miracle of this Ummah which Allāh has granted us. Everything is recorded. It prevents anybody else coming with new statements and new things as they would have no chain of narration for them.

'Abdullāh ibn al-Mubarak said: "Isnād (having a chain of narration) is part of the dīn. If there were no such thing, anybody would say as they pleased."

'Isnād' means 'to rest upon' or 'to support'. A person who has an isnād/chain of narration, is supported by a number of scholars who came before him. His views are more likely to be identical to the views of those that came before him. There is a shared commonality with the previous generations. A person without isnād has no support from the previous generations. Very likely, he will be 'supporting himself' and have his own views and ideas. He may be identical in some issues to the earlier scholars and Ṣa#ābah, but very possibly he will be in serious error.

Hence, people who do not have isnād and have not studied under scholars can very possibly create their own understandings and conclusions and can be different to the Ṣa#ābah and earlier scholars. That is why we should always refer to or consult with scholars who have been educated in the classical mode and who therefore beneﬁt from a sound isnād. This will save us from the negative inﬂuences of all the different and strange things people are saying in the name of Islām. We need to follow the Islām practised by the Ṣa#ābah. Those scholars who can call on an isnād have 'Aqā'id a connection with the Ṣa#ābah. Between them and the Ṣa#ābah, there are a number of great scholars with whom they are soundly connected.

Shortness of this Life First we have 'lahw,' an Arabic term which means a play without any motive. When we were younger we played with toys without any purpose. This is the stage of 'lahw.' Thereafter a child grows up and now his or her play is 'la'ib': purposeful play motivated by aspirations to win and to rise to a challenge. The games are for a trophy, medal or to stay at the top of the league. Can you remember the times when you used to play with your toys? Look how fast that time has gone. Now the rivalry steps up another level... Who's driving the latest car? Who has the best job? How many children has she got?

Shortness of this Life The Qur'ānic verse mentioned contains a metaphor which should inspire us to reﬂect on all these stages in our short lives. Allāh invites us to reﬂect on our surroundings. This child (nurtured plant) then grows into the 'summer' of life, giving the appearance of health and vitality. By using the metaphor of crops, Allāh wants us to see just how quickly this verdant pasture turns into the yellows of autumn. 'Autumn' in terms of the life cycle of humans is that time when they lose the strength and vitality that came to them in their young adult years. Just as the plants and crops wither and bend quickly, similarly we humans become of the people who have passed away and been forgotten. To use another comparison, we are but words on a page in the pages of the history of mankind. These pages are turning quickly, the story is moving deceptively fast. One moment we are taking our turn amongst our peers, living in brick houses, and driving in metal cars that ﬂash and glimmer in the sunlight, the next moment our houses are made of simple wooden caskets that ﬁt six feet under the ground. All that we aspired for will be left here in this world, if we forget our real purpose.

He says clearly: 'And in the Hereafter there is a severe punishment (for the disbelievers), and forgiveness from Allāh and (Allāh 's) pleasure (for the believers and the righteous)'. Depending on how we spent our short time in this world we will be rewarded or punished. The worldly life is nothing but a material of delusion. In such a short life, one that is becoming shorter as we read each sentence, what are we doing to guarantee ourselves a pleasurable ﬁnal home? As each second passes by, are we working hard for the toy cars and the brick houses, and are we preferring them over the houses made of gold, encrusted with ﬁne rubies and precious stones, such as those Allāh has promised to the inhabitants of Jannah? A saint once said that he understood the shortness of this life by understanding the image of watching a man selling a block of ice on a hot day. Shortness of this Life Imām Ghazāli narrates a story about a man who takes a walk in the jungle. The man looks down into the well and is shocked to see a serpent with its mouth wide open and ready to bite, waiting for the man to fall so it can devour him. All three are bringing him closer to an inevitable end. There is no way out. He twists his neck to look behind him and sees a bees nest attached to the wall of the well. His pressing reality remains the same, though, as none of the animals will disappear. The lion is death. It is always there, always waiting for the moment at which to take our life. The white mouse is the day, and the black mouse is the night, both eating away at our time. We can't bring back seconds, minutes, hours or days. What the mice have eaten is gone for good. Yet every second that we cling on to that rope is necessary. Clinging on to the rope is the only thing that can save us temporarily until the mornings and the nights come to an end and our fall arrives.

Shortness of this Life The serpent is our grave, the place of rest if we fall or let go of the rope eaten away by the mice. The honeycomb and the sweetness brought by its taste, is this World, this worldly life. It is temporary and its pleasures are there only for a small amount of time. Soon enough the sweetness is gone. When the sweetness disappears, we realise exactly how much time we have left before the mice ﬁnish eating and we fall prey to the serpent. And no matter how long we may close our eyes, dear students, they will always be there.

Taqwā

The literal meaning of 'taqwā' is to abstain. When it is used in conjunction with Allāh , taqwā means to abstain for the fear of Allāh , to have the awareness that Allāh is watching us at all times which inevitably will result in one abstaining from sins.

Taqwā is one of the greatest qualities a believer can have and has been mentioned in the Qur'ān numerous times. The believers have been commanded to have taqwā in all aspects of their lives.

Taqwā is to abstain from everything that Allāh has forbidden for us. When a person abandons a life of sin he adopts a certain level of taqwā and therefore becomes conscious that there is an upper authority to which he will have to answer. A person who leads a life of taqwā and abandons a life of sin will truly experience a wholesome life in this world and true happiness in the Ākhirah. A person who craves the fulﬁlment of all his desires may seem to be happy outwardly, they may experience the thrill or the pleasures of one sin or another, but the inner happiness is removed from their life.

The cost of leading a life of sin is much greater than the quick temporary pleasure felt at the time of indulgence. A great many people have spent their lives following their desires, thinking that they will eventually be satisﬁed, but the end result is that they become engulfed in a miserable web. As we know, a web is a sticky, tangling trap. The greatest harm a person does when they commit a sin is that they break the connection with their Creator. They should ask themselves if the materialistic pleasure is really worth it. The owner of happiness and success is Allāh . A person may purchase the most expensive bed but he cannot buy the sleep that he supposes he will be able to have with that bed. Indeed, the gift of sleep comes from the Owner of peace and rest: Allāh .

You can have the most good-looking, intelligent spouse but you cannot have the love from that person unless the Owner of love places love between you. When the Owner of everything is none other than Allāh then ask yourself why are you watching those things you watch? Why are you listening to those tracks you listen to? Is it for a pleasure that truly lasts? Taqwā teaches us that Allāh 's displeasure is something that we should be careful to avoid.

If and when a sin does occur we must not sit and 'stew' on it, rather we should repent to Allāh immediately and start anew. When a person tries their best to stay away from sins and leads a life of taqwā, Allāh blesses them immensely.

"Whoever fears Allāh, He brings forth a way out for him, and provides him (with what he needs) from where he cannot even imagine." (Qur'ān 65:2-3)

Many a time we get into situations where we see no practical way out or no solution. Allāh promises a person who has taqwā that He will bring a way out from every difﬁcult situation. Sub#ānallāh!

Today many people are worried about their provision and sustenance. Often we make a great fuss about household budgets and what earnings we feel we 'need' to sustain our quality of life. By leading a life of taqwā, Allāh will grant us the comforts and provisions from where we least expect them.

Allāh says:

"O you who believe, if you fear Allāh, He will provide you with a Criterion (to distinguish between right and wrong) and will write off your evil deeds and will forgive you. Allāh is the Lord of great bounty." (Qur'ān 8:29)

Allāh will grant a special gift for those who abstain from sins: the ability to distinguish between good and evil.

Taqwā, then, is something that is needed to guide us through life, to give us conﬁdence, and to make the many decisions which will affect our families and communities. If we lead a life of taqwā, Allāh will grant us that distinct ability to choose right from wrong in all matters.

There are many ways to gain taqwā.

One of the easiest ways, the Qur'ān tells us:

"O you who believe, the fasts have been enjoined upon you as they were enjoined upon those before you, so that you may be God-fearing." (Qur'ān 2:183)

Fasting is a great way to create that self restraint in the body. The food is there but the fasting person will abstain. They are learning to control their desires.

Practice of this will allow them to control the 'nafs' when invited to temptation. Our Beloved Messenger Muḥammad said:

"Whosoever leaves something for the sake of Allāh, then Allāh will grant that person something better." (Aḥmad) So anytime you abandon a sin and you reject the invitation to engage in the disobedience of Allāh , then remember you are making yourself eligible for all the above blessings. How long will the temptation last for? Steer clear of it and control yourself, and you'll see the blessings ﬂood into your life. You will become the happiest person.

Our Beloved Messenger Muḥammad said:

"Three persons of a people who came before you were on a journey when they were overtaken by a storm and therefore they took shelter in a cave. A rock slipped down from the mountain and blocked the exit from the cave.

One of them said: 'The only way for deliverance left is to beseech Allāh in the name of some virtuous deed.' Thereupon one of them supplicated, 'O Lord, I had a cousin whom I loved more passionately than any man loves a woman. I tried to seduce her but she would have none of me, till a season of great hardship due to famine, she approached me (for help) and I gave her one hundred and twenty dinars on condition that she would engage in sin with me. O Lord, if I did this thing seeking only your pleasure, then please relieve us of the distress in which we ﬁnd ourselves.' (Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī)

Together with the other two people's prayers, the rock moved enough for them to exit. May Allāh allow us to protect our desires and lead a life of taqwā.

  • Abandoning that particular sin immediately
  • Making a ﬁrm promise never to return to that

Debates and Discussions The intention of debate should be to reach the correct conclusion and understanding.

One should never debate to prove how clever one is. A person should debate issues after researching them thoroughly. To debate on a subject without having knowledge of it is foolishness.

Before, during and after, one should check the intention for debating.

One should not get angry whilst debating.

One should not use any foul language when debating.

One should not try to overcome the other person by raising one's voice.

One should discuss things calmly and present strong proofs for one's point.

One should always listen to what the other person Ādābhas to say clearly and properly before reacting. It is not necessary that the other person accepts your point.

One's goal when debating should be to defend one's stance and get the message across.
