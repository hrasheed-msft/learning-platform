Fiqh

Water

📝 Lesson Summary

When a person wants to pray to Allāh by engaging in ṣalāh, ṭawāf, sajdah tilāwah or even for the purpose of holding the Qur'ān, they must be pure and in the state of wuḍū'. When a person wants to recite the Qur'ān without touching it they can do so without wuḍū', however they must not be in the state of major impurity. The way to become pure is to perform wuḍū' or ghusl.

Key Points:

  • The way to become pure is to perform wuḍū' or ghusl.
  • Small water is that which covers a lesser area than the above amount.
  • Pure water is regarded as 'used' the moment it separates from the body.
  • For example, a person who is washing their arm will make the water 'used' the moment it separates from the arm.

Maturity

📝 Lesson Summary

Masā'il of reaching maturity As a girl matures, her body develops and some physical changes take place. One of the biggest changes is the releasing of blood from the private part. Women have three types of bleeding: 1.Ḥay} (Period/Menstruation) 2.Istiḥāḍah (dysfunctional uterine bleeding) 3.Nifās (post-natal bleeding) Ḥay} - The monthly period A period, also called menstruation, is blood which ﬂows from the womb and exits the middle passage of the private parts.

Key Points:

  • One of the biggest changes is the releasing of blood from the private part.
  • The Islamic term for this is #ay}.
  • Once a girl over nine experiences this type of bleeding, she is classed as mature in Islam and is accountable for her own actions.
  • This maturity is called being bālighah.
  • Age of #ay} A girl will not get #ay} before the age of 9 lunar years which is equivalent to 8 years and 9 months according to the Gregorian calendar.

Karāmāt

📝 Lesson Summary

The Ahlus Sunnah believe that the close friends of Allāh can also display extraordinary acts which are unusual. These acts are known as karāmāt. By having these acts performed by them, Allāh intends to honour and strengthen them. Envy

📝 Lesson Summary

'Or, is it that they are envious of people over what Allāh has given them of His bounty…' (Qur'ān 4:54) Ḥasan narrates that our Beloved Messenger Muḥammad said: "Beware of envy for it eats up good deeds just as ﬁre eats up wood" (Abū Dāwūd) Envy or jealousy is to wish a particular blessing be taken away from another person. Envy was the ﬁrst sin committed in the Heavens and the ﬁrst sin committed on Earth. Shayṭān was envious of Ādam which led him to deny the command of Allāh .

Key Points:

  • Shayṭān was envious of Ādam which led him to deny the command of Allāh .
  • Envy creates enmity between blood relatives: it will make a person disobey Allāh and a person at times stoops so low that they forget themselves.
  • An envious person is always angry.
  • An envious person forgets the favours Allāh has blessed them with and begins to burn inside when they see others who have been blessed.
  • It is Allāh 's distribution that they are questioning!

Pride

📝 Lesson Summary

Many people become confused as to the actual meaning of this word. Some think that a person that dresses smartly does so out of pride, while others mistakenly assume that a person who talks very eloquently has pride. Let us look at the deﬁnition given to us by the saviour of mankind Muḥammad . He said pride is "rejecting the truth and looking down on people." (Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim) In another ḥadīth our Beloved Messenger said: "He who has an atom's weight of pride in his heart shall not enter Jannah.

Key Points:

  • It is totally normal to make mistakes.
  • Indeed, accepting and learning from our mistakes is what makes us better each day.
  • The second point is looking down on others.
  • Thinking of another human being as being beneath us because of his race, colour, ethnicity or disability is a sign of pride.
  • Allāh is the Creator of all.

Modesty in Dress

📝 Lesson Summary

Modesty is a very good quality in Islām. Modesty brings goodness into a person's life. Modesty is having a sense of shame and respect for oneself. Modesty is to be aware of Allāh at all times and know He is looking at you and to act, dress, speak and think in the manner He likes.

Key Points:

  • Modesty is a very good quality in Islām.
  • Modesty is having a sense of shame and respect for oneself.
  • Modesty is to be aware of Allāh at all times and know He is looking at you and to act, dress, speak and think in the manner He likes.
  • Similarly, Allāh is always watching us.
  • If we have modesty in our lives, we will only act, dress or talk in the way Allāh likes.

Key Points:

  • The hands and ﬁngers are the main point of contact with everything around us and are therefore exposed to many germs.
  • Outward cleanliness will have an effect on our inner thoughts and feelings.
