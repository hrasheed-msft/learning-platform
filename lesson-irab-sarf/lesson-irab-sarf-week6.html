Part 1: Sarf Warm-up
10 minutes · أفعال الناقص الواوي واليائي
▼

This week trains naaqis verbs where the final radical is weak. You will drill دَعَا يَدْعُو and رَمَى يَرْمِي, then apply the rule of حذف حرف العلة في الجزم before reading the passage on المطلق والمقيد.

💡 ناقص واوي — دَعَا يَدْعُو In jussive and imperative, the final weak letter drops: لَمْ يَدْعُ، اُدْعُ.

Drill A — Conjugate دَعَا:

الضميرالماضيالمضارعالمجزوم / الأمر
هُوَدَعَا
أَنْتَدَعَوْتَتَدْعُو
هُمْلَمْ يَدْعُوا
💡 ناقص يائي — رَمَى يَرْمِي The yā’ becomes alif in perfect (رَمَى), then drops in jussive/imperative: لَمْ يَرْمِ، اِرْمِ.

Drill B — Conjugate رَمَى:

Week 6 Key Takeaways

I'rab mastery targets
  • Differentiate حال (state) from تمييز (clarifier).
  • Both are منصوب نكرة, but their semantic roles differ.
  • In legal prose, short منصوب words often need contextual judgment.
Sarf mastery targets
  • Naqis verbs lose final weak letter in jussive and imperative.
  • Watch for final ى / ا clues in unvowelled text.
  • Pair morphology with syntax: form changes often affect I'rab visibility.
